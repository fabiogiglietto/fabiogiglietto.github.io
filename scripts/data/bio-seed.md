Fabio Giglietto is Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Università di Urbino Carlo Bo, where he teaches Generative AI and Media and Digital Social Network Analysis. He earned his doctorate at the same institution. His scientific activity is located at the intersection of computational social science, analysis of digital platforms and political communication, with a specific focus on the study of information disorders and coordinated behaviour on social media.

He is the founder and coordinator of the **Mapping Italian News Research Program (MINE)** — <https://mine.uniurb.it/>. MINE is a research programme active at the University of Urbino since 2017 that investigates Italian media coverage and the impact of social media on electoral processes, public opinion and health information. Over the years MINE has hosted a succession of externally funded sub-projects: **Mapping Italian News — Elezioni 2018** (the seed project of MINE, 2017–2018, funded by the Foundation Open Society Institute in cooperation with the Information Program of the Open Society Foundations; website <https://elezioni2018.news/>); **MINE-SMD — Patterns of Facebook Interactions around Insular and Cross-Partisan Media Sources in the Run-up to the 2018 Italian Election** (2019–2021, funded by the Social Science Research Council under the Social Media and Democracy Research Grants programme, with access to Facebook data via Social Science One); **MINE-FACTS** on COVID-19 fact-checking tools (Italian MUR, FISR 2020, project code FISR2020IP_00327e); *Mapping Coordinated Networks That Circulate Problematic Health Content in India and Nigeria* (Gates Foundation grant INV-004543-02 via the Media Ecosystems Analysis Group); a study on coordinated behaviour in the 2021 German Federal Election (Media Authority of North Rhine-Westphalia); and two further EU-funded projects — **vera.ai** (Horizon Europe) and **PROMPT** (European Commission, DG CNECT) — detailed below. Within MINE, Giglietto co-developed **CooRnet** (with Nicola Righetti and Luca Rossi), an open-source R package that introduced the *Coordinated Link Sharing Behavior* (CLSB) detection method for Facebook and Instagram. CooRnet was adopted internationally by researchers, investigative journalists and fact-checkers, and applied in electoral contexts in Italy, Germany, Brazil, Nigeria and India. Following the shutdown of Meta's CrowdTangle platform on 14 August 2024 — the data source on which CooRnet depended — the package is now **discontinued at the operational level**; the underlying CLSB methodology it introduced, however, continues to inform subsequent tools, notably the **CooRTweet** R package (Righetti & Balluff, 2023–), an independent, platform- and content-agnostic implementation of coordinated-behaviour detection maintained by Nicola Righetti at Uniurb DISCUI (the same department as the PI) and Paul Balluff at GESIS. CooRnet is cited as a methodological reference in Bruns et al.'s (2025) *practice mapping* paper in *Social Media + Society*.

On the European competitive-funding front, under the MINE programme Giglietto served as **WP4 Leader** of the Horizon Europe project **vera.ai — VERification Assisted by Artificial Intelligence** (Grant ID: 101070093, 2022 – October 2025, **concluded**), which developed AI-based tools for countering disinformation. He also contributed as partner to the European Commission project **PROMPT** (Predictive Research On Misinformation and Narratives Propagation Trajectories, funded by DG CNECT under grant agreement CNECT/LC-02629302, 2023 – February 2026, **concluded**), dedicated to monitoring disinformation narratives in Europe. All externally funded MINE sub-projects listed above have now concluded; the MINE research programme itself remains active as the institutional and intellectual umbrella for the PI's ongoing research line, with no active external grant at present.

He is a member of the International Communication Association (ICA), the Association of Internet Researchers (AoIR) and the Italian Association for Political Communication. He has participated in the board of Research Committee 51 on Sociocybernetics of the International Sociological Association.

### Software tools and resources developed

- **CooRnet** (R package; Giglietto, Righetti & Rossi, 2020): introduced the Coordinated Link Sharing Behavior (CLSB) detection method on Facebook/Instagram — 75 stars on GitHub, adopted in international research and investigative reports. **Discontinued at the operational level** following the shutdown of Meta's CrowdTangle on 14 August 2024, on which the package relied for data access. The CLSB methodology it introduced continues to inform downstream tools; CooRnet is cited in Bruns et al. (2025).
- **CooRTweet** (Righetti & Balluff, 2023–): independent R package that builds on CooRnet's CLSB methodology to provide platform- and content-agnostic detection of coordinated behaviour on social media. Maintained by Nicola Righetti (Uniurb DISCUI — same department as the PI) and Paul Balluff (GESIS). Does not depend on CrowdTangle and works with current APIs (Twitter/X, Meta Content Library, and more). Actively maintained; latest CRAN release 2.1.3 (March 2025). Methodological paper: Righetti & Balluff (2025), *Computational Communication Research* 7(1). Giglietto is not a co-developer.

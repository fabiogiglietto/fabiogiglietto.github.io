Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished academic specializing in Internet Studies and currently serves as an Associate Professor at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's research primarily focuses on the dynamic relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly work has been widely published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society, reflecting his significant contributions to the field.

Professor Giglietto's extensive body of work includes influential publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has been cited nearly 400 times, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His research on the manipulation of media during the Italian elections and the circulation of false information within the hybrid news system has been pivotal in understanding the complexities of digital communication and media manipulation.

Beyond his publications, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in exploring the intersections of social media analysis and disinformation. His recent research projects continue to address the challenges posed by digital platforms in shaping public discourse. Engaging with the academic community, he frequently shares insights and developments from his work on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he discusses the implications of his research on computational social science and media studies.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto is involved in various professional activities, including receiving grants that support his innovative research. His work not only advances academic knowledge but also informs public understanding of the role of digital technologies in modern society. As a thought leader in Internet Studies, Fabio Giglietto remains committed to exploring the evolving landscape of digital communication and its impact on society.

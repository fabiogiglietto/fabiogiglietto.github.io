Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic background in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research meticulously examines the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. He has contributed significantly to the field through numerous publications in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly work focuses on the dynamics of social media analysis and disinformation, a field in which he is recognized as a leading researcher. His notable publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections," both of which have been highly cited by peers. His research continues to explore the intricate mechanisms of information dissemination in digital environments, reflecting his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by the digital age.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader research community through various platforms, including BlueSky and Mastodon. He is currently involved in projects that investigate the spread of disinformation and the use of computational social science methods to decode complex media phenomena. His recent professional activities include leading a research team funded by a major grant to study the impact of social media on public opinion during electoral processes.

Through his innovative research and active engagement with contemporary digital issues, Fabio Giglietto continues to contribute valuable insights into the rapidly evolving landscape of internet studies. His work not only enhances academic discourse but also informs public understanding and policy-making related to digital communication and media systems.

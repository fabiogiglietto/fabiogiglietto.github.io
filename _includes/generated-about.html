Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is a renowned Professor of Internet Studies within the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As an Associate Professor, he brings a wealth of expertise and passion to the field, particularly in understanding the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research is deeply rooted in the theory of information and communication, with a specific focus on how these elements shape society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the academic community through his extensive publications in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His impactful work, including studies on the dynamics of social media participation and the dissemination of false information within hybrid news systems, has garnered over 1,700 citations, reflecting his influence in the field. Notably, his research on coordinated media manipulation during Italian elections exemplifies his commitment to exploring critical issues of disinformation and media integrity.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research on social media analysis and computational social science, with a keen interest in the mechanisms of disinformation. His active involvement in professional networks and platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon highlights his dedication to staying at the forefront of emerging digital communication trends. Through his work, he continues to contribute valuable insights into the evolving digital landscape, influencing both academic discourse and practical applications in media studies.

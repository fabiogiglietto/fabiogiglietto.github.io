Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His research explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, with a focus on the theory of information, communication, and society. His work seeks to understand how digital platforms influence public opinion and democratic processes.

Professor Giglietto's research employs digital methods, computational social science, and social media analysis to investigate phenomena such as coordinated behavior, information disorder, and the impact of algorithms on online discourse. He leads the Mapping Italian News Research Program (MINE), which investigates Italian news media coverage and the impact of social media on Italian elections. He is also involved in the EU-funded Horizon Europe project VERAAI, developing AI-based tools for journalists and fact-checkers to verify multimedia content and combat disinformation, with a focus on detecting coordinated behavior and inauthentic networks. Additionally, he contributes to the EU-funded PROMPT initiative, which addresses disinformation narratives across Europe.

Professor Giglietto's publications have appeared in leading journals, including the *Journal of Communication*, *Information, Communication & Society*, *the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media*, *Social Media + Society*, and *Social Science Computer Review*. His recent work includes an analysis of Meta's political content reduction policy and its impact on the reach of Italian parliamentarians on Facebook. He teaches courses such as Generative AI and Media, and Digital Social Network Analysis.

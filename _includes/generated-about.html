Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished scholar and Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, his research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society. Dr. Giglietto’s expertise is widely recognized, with extensive publications in reputable journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His work has been pivotal in understanding the dynamics of media manipulation and the dissemination of information in the digital age.

Dr. Giglietto's influential research includes pivotal studies like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant academic attention with nearly 400 citations. His contributions extend to exploring the role of social media platforms as research data sources, as well as analyzing the spread of disinformation within hybrid news systems. His paper "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system" stands out as a critical examination of the mechanisms behind the spread of false information.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is actively engaged in research projects that utilize computational social science methodologies to analyze social media behavior and disinformation. His recent endeavors explore coordinated link-sharing behavior during political events, offering insights into media manipulation strategies. He frequently shares his research findings and professional insights on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he engages with a community of scholars and practitioners interested in social media analysis and digital communication.

Beyond his research, Professor Giglietto contributes to the academic community through teaching and mentorship at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His courses on Internet Studies equip students with the critical skills needed to navigate and understand the evolving digital landscape. Through his dedication to both research and education, Dr. Giglietto continues to shape the discourse on media and technology, influencing both academic circles and public understanding of digital media’s impact on society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he teaches within the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. Renowned for his extensive research in the fields of information theory, communication, and society, Dr. Giglietto focuses particularly on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work has been widely published in leading academic journals, including the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society, reflecting his profound impact on the field.

Throughout his academic career, Dr. Giglietto has contributed significantly to the understanding of digital media's role in modern society. His notable publications such as "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source" have collectively garnered over 1,750 citations. These works explore the dynamic interactions within social media platforms and their implications on media consumption and dissemination, particularly during pivotal events such as elections and crises.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is deeply involved in research projects that examine the spread of disinformation and the manipulation of media through coordinated online behaviors. His recent studies on the Italian elections demonstrate the complex networks of link sharing and the impact of false information within hybrid news systems. Actively engaging with the academic community, he shares insights and developments from his research on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, fostering dialogue on the challenges and opportunities presented by digital technologies in contemporary communication.

Dr. Giglietto's contributions extend beyond academia as he collaborates on interdisciplinary projects that address societal challenges related to media and technology. His leadership in internet studies not only advances scholarly discourse but also informs public policy and media literacy initiatives. Through his ongoing research and public engagement, Fabio Giglietto continues to shape the understanding of digital communication in the 21st century.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a rich background in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's research primarily explores the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions are widely recognized, featuring in leading academic journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's prolific body of work includes significant publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered notable academic attention. His research is driven by a keen interest in understanding how digital technologies influence public discourse and media manipulation, as evidenced by his studies on false information circulation within hybrid news systems and coordinated media manipulation during elections.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader research community through platforms such as LinkedIn and BlueSky, where he shares insights into his current work on social media analysis and disinformation. His recent projects reflect a commitment to advancing computational social science and understanding the societal implications of digital media. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he continues to inspire through both his teaching and his groundbreaking research initiatives.

Professor Giglietto's work is supported by prestigious grants, and he frequently participates in international conferences to share his findings. His dedication to exploring the complexities of digital communication ensures that his contributions remain at the forefront of Internet Studies, offering valuable perspectives on the evolving landscape of media and society.

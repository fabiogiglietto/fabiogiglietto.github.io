Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he serves within the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. As an expert in the field of Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto focuses his research on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly work has been widely recognized, with publications in leading journals like the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the understanding of how information circulates in the digital age, specifically exploring phenomena such as disinformation and media manipulation. His seminal paper, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has been cited extensively, reflecting his impact on the field. Additionally, his recent research projects have delved into the dynamics of coordinated link sharing behavior during political elections and the role of false information in hybrid news systems.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research on social media analysis and computational social science, with special attention to the mechanisms of disinformation. He actively shares insights and developments from his research through platforms like BlueSky, where he is known for his thought-provoking discussions on emerging digital phenomena. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he is dedicated to advancing the academic discourse on digital communication, fostering a deeper understanding of the digital transformations shaping contemporary society.

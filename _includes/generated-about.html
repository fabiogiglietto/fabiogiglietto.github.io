Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he serves in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities, and International Studies. With a profound dedication to understanding the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in the field of Internet Studies. His scholarly contributions are extensively published in prominent journals like the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's research primarily delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular emphasis on the impact and interplay of social media and digital platforms. Notable among his influential works are "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," both of which have garnered significant citations. His recent projects continue to explore themes of disinformation and computational social science, reflecting his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in media manipulation and misinformation.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is actively engaged in cutting-edge research on social media analysis, focusing on the dissemination and effects of disinformation across digital platforms. His expertise is frequently sought after in discussions on how digital technologies shape public discourse and media consumption. In addition to his academic pursuits, Dr. Giglietto regularly shares insights and updates on his ongoing projects via professional networks, including BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he contributes to the broader conversation on the evolving landscape of Internet studies.

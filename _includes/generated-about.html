Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated passion for understanding the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has become a leading voice in the theory of information, communication, and society. He shares his insights and knowledge through courses on Internet Studies, inspiring the next generation of scholars and practitioners.

His extensive body of research has been widely recognized and published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Among his notable works is the influential study "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant academic attention with 397 citations. Professor Giglietto's research frequently explores the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and the political implications of digital communication, as evidenced by his critical analysis of coordinated link-sharing behavior during recent Italian elections.

Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in current research projects that delve into the role of social media in the dissemination of information and the multifaceted nature of 'fake news' within today's hybrid news systems. His recent endeavors also include collaborative studies on computational social science, reflecting his commitment to advancing knowledge in the digital age. Through platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, he shares updates on his latest findings and engages with a global community of scholars and professionals.

As an Associate Professor at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto continues to contribute to the academic community through his innovative research and dedication to teaching. His work not only enhances our understanding of digital media landscapes but also informs policy and practice in the ever-evolving field of communication sciences.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he contributes significantly to the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With an extensive academic background, Professor Giglietto delves into the intricate theory of information, communication, and society. His research primarily focuses on the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Over the years, he has enriched the academic community with numerous publications in renowned journals, including the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's work is at the forefront of understanding digital landscapes, as evidenced by his widely cited studies such as "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory." His research provides critical insights into how social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube serve as vital data sources for societal analysis. Recently, his projects have focused on the spread of disinformation within hybrid news systems and the examination of coordinated media manipulation, particularly in the context of political events like the Italian elections.

As an active contributor to current discussions on social media analysis and computational social science, Professor Giglietto engages with the academic community through various platforms. His recent professional activities highlight ongoing research into disinformation and its societal impacts. Through collaborations and publications, he continues to explore new methodologies for analyzing digital communication phenomena. His ongoing projects are an integral part of advancing the field and fostering a deeper understanding of the digital world's complexity.

Beyond his research, Professor Giglietto is committed to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars, ensuring they are equipped with the skills necessary to navigate and influence the ever-evolving digital landscape. His dedication to education and research at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo underscores his pivotal role in shaping contemporary Internet Studies.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He teaches Internet Studies and Social Media Analysis. Professor Giglietto's work explores the relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, focusing on the theory of information, communication, and society.

His research delves into the impact of digital platforms on social interaction, with a keen interest in disinformation and the role of social media in shaping public opinion. Recent projects focus on social media analysis, computational social science, and the detection of disinformation campaigns, particularly in political communication. Professor Giglietto is involved in the EU-funded PROMPT initiative, which addresses disinformation narratives across Europe. He also leads the Mapping Italian News Research Program (MINE), investigating Italian news media coverage and the impact of social media on Italian elections.

Professor Giglietto has published extensively in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, Social Media + Society, and Social Science Computer Review. His work includes pivotal insights into coordinated link-sharing behavior and media manipulation. According to Google Scholar, his publications have been cited over 2200 times. He actively engages with academic and professional communities on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, fostering dialogue around the evolving landscape of Internet Studies.

Professor Giglietto's research contributes significantly to understanding the complexities of the digital world. He is a leading developer at Coornet.org and a WP4 leader at Veraai.eu. His work has revealed errors in Facebook's data, highlighting the need for critical data verification in social media research.

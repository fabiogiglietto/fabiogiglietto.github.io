Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he serves within the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a profound interest in the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in the field of communication theory and information society. His work, which frequently explores the dynamics of social media and its impact on public discourse, has been published in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto is particularly interested in the phenomena of disinformation and the mechanisms through which false information spreads within hybrid news systems. His notable publications include "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system" and "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections". These works underscore his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by contemporary digital communication environments. His current research projects continue to delve into these critical issues, leveraging computational social science techniques to analyze social media platforms.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community and the public through various social media platforms, including BlueSky and Mastodon. His recent posts highlight ongoing studies on social media analytics and disinformation, reflecting his dedication to advancing the field through collaborative research efforts and public discourse. With a total of 20 publications and 1753 citations, his contributions have significantly influenced the academic landscape, informing both theoretical frameworks and practical approaches to media studies.

Fabio Giglietto remains a pivotal figure in Internet Studies, continually exploring the boundaries of information theory and societal communication. His academic endeavors are complemented by his teaching role at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he inspires the next generation of scholars to critically engage with the digital world. Through his research, Professor Giglietto not only advances academic knowledge but also contributes to broader societal conversations about the role of media and technology in shaping contemporary life.

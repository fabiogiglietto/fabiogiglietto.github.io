Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research primarily explores the complex interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions are widely recognized, with his work featured in prominent journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, among others.

Throughout his academic career, Professor Giglietto has made significant strides in understanding the dynamics of social media and its implications on society. His notable publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have collectively garnered over 1700 citations. His research extends into the realm of disinformation and the manipulation of media, as evidenced by his work on coordinated link-sharing behavior during Italian elections.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that delve into social media analysis and the spread of disinformation, leveraging computational social science methodologies. His latest endeavors are shared on BlueSky and Mastodon, providing insights into his ongoing research activities and collaborations. As an Associate Professor, he continues to contribute to the academic community and mentor the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies.

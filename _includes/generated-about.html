Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-rooted passion for understanding the intricacies of digital communication, he specializes in the theory of information, communication, and society. His research extensively explores the dynamic relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, a focus that has led to numerous publications in highly regarded journals such as the Journal of Communication and Information, Communication & Society.

Professor Giglietto's academic contributions are highlighted by his influential publications, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant citations. His work on the manipulation of media during Italian elections and the propagation of false information within the hybrid news system underscores his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in media and communication. With over 1,753 citations across 20 publications, his research has profoundly impacted the field of Internet studies.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that delve into social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His ongoing research seeks to unravel the complex mechanisms through which digital platforms influence public discourse and societal perceptions. His recent activity on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon reflects his dedication to leveraging social media for scholarly collaboration and discussion on critical issues surrounding digital communication and misinformation.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto not only contributes to academic excellence through his research but also inspires the next generation of scholars by teaching Internet Studies. His dedication to both research and education exemplifies his commitment to advancing knowledge and understanding in the rapidly evolving digital landscape.

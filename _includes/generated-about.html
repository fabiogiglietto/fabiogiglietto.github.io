Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies (DISCUI) at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He teaches courses in Internet Studies and Social Media Analysis. His research explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, with a focus on the theory of information, communication, and society.

Professor Giglietto's current research delves into the impact of digital platforms on social interaction, with a keen interest in disinformation and the role of social media in shaping public opinion. He is actively involved in projects focusing on social media analysis, computational social science, and the detection of disinformation campaigns, particularly in political communication. Notably, he contributes to the EU-funded PROMPT initiative, which addresses disinformation narratives across Europe. He also leads the Mapping Italian News Research Program (MINE), investigating Italian news media coverage and the impact of social media on Italian elections. Recent work includes exploration of the Digital Services Act (DSA) and its implications for social media research. He actively engages with research on coordinated inauthentic behavior and manipulation on social media.

His publications have appeared in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, Social Media + Society, and Social Science Computer Review. According to Google Scholar, his work has been cited over 2200 times. He is a leading developer at Coornet.org and a WP4 leader at Veraai.eu. Professor Giglietto actively engages with academic and professional communities on platforms like BlueSky (@fabiogiglietto.bsky.social) and Mastodon (@fabiogiglietto@aoir.social), fostering dialogue around the evolving landscape of Internet Studies.

Professor Giglietto's research contributes significantly to understanding the complexities of the digital world. His recent work involves analyzing social media platform policies and coordinated activities, including the use of AI in research and the detection of bots. He is dedicated to exploring novel methods for analyzing digital social environments and critically interpreting research results.

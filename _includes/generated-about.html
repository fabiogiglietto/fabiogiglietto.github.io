Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey is marked by a profound engagement with the theory of information, communication, and society, especially focusing on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto's research has been pivotal in understanding the dynamics of social media interactions, disinformation, and computational social science, making significant contributions to renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly oeuvre includes over 20 publications with a total of 1,753 citations, underscoring his influence in the field. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant academic attention. Additionally, his research on the manipulation of media during the Italian elections and the proliferation of 'fake news' within hybrid news systems has been instrumental in unraveling the complexities of media consumption in the digital age. His ongoing projects continue to explore these themes, utilizing innovative methodologies to address contemporary challenges in media studies.

Beyond his publication record, Professor Giglietto is actively involved in leading-edge research projects that delve into the analysis of social media platforms, the spread of misinformation, and their societal impacts. His recent engagements include collaborative initiatives that harness data from platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube to better understand user behaviors and media manipulation strategies. These projects not only advance academic knowledge but also provide actionable insights for practitioners and policymakers.

Professor Giglietto's professional activities extend to active participation in the academic community through platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he shares insights on his latest research endeavors and engages with fellow academics and industry professionals. His contributions have been recognized through various grants and awards, reflecting his commitment to advancing the field of internet studies and contributing to the broader discourse on digital media and communication.

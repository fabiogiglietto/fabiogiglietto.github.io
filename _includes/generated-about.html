Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound interest in the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto's research primarily explores the theory of information, communication, and society. His scholarly work has been published in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, contributing significantly to the academic discourse on these subjects.

In addition to his teaching responsibilities, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in research projects that investigate the impact of social media on public discourse and the dissemination of disinformation. His recent work delves into coordinated link-sharing behavior and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems, reflecting his commitment to understanding and addressing contemporary challenges in media and communication. His contributions to the field are evidenced by the substantial citation count of his publications, which include highly influential papers such as "Second Screen and Participation" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar."

Professor Giglietto is also an active participant in the academic community through various platforms, sharing insights and collaborating with fellow researchers. His recent engagements on professional networks like BlueSky (@fabiogiglietto.bsky.social), Mastodon, and LinkedIn highlight his ongoing projects and thought leadership in social media analysis and computational social science. Through these endeavors, he continues to advance the understanding of digital communication phenomena and their implications for society.

With a total of 20 publications and over 1750 citations, Professor Giglietto's academic contributions have garnered international recognition. His research not only informs scholarly work but also provides valuable insights for policymakers and practitioners navigating the complexities of the digital age. As an Associate Professor, he remains dedicated to fostering an environment of academic excellence and innovation at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo.

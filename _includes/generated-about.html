Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His scholarly work primarily explores the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular emphasis on the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As a seasoned academic, Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the field, with his research findings being published in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has garnered acclaim for his work on social media analysis and disinformation. His highly cited publications, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," reflect his deep engagement with the complexities of digital platforms. His ongoing research delves into the proliferation of false information within hybrid news systems, as highlighted in his recent publications on coordinated media manipulation and discursive strategies post-major events.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively involved in research projects examining the impact of social media on public discourse and the spread of disinformation, leveraging computational social science methodologies. His work is supported by various grants, underscoring his position as a leading figure in the study of digital communication phenomena. Engaging with the academic community, he frequently shares insights and developments from his research on platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he discusses the implications of his findings for media dynamics and societal structures.

Professor Giglietto continues to inspire students and colleagues alike through his teaching and mentorship at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His commitment to advancing our understanding of the digital age is evident in both his academic endeavors and his active participation in scholarly discussions on emerging trends in internet studies. Through his work, he aims to contribute to a more nuanced comprehension of how digital technologies shape and are shaped by societal interactions.

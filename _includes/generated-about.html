Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He has carved a niche in the field of Internet Studies, where he not only imparts knowledge but also conducts pioneering research. His academic pursuits are deeply rooted in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a keen focus on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His contributions to the field are well-documented, with publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly works reflect his profound insights into contemporary media phenomena. Notable among his publications is "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant attention and citations. His research extends to exploring the dynamics of misinformation, as evidenced by his paper on the hybrid news system's role in the dissemination of false information. His scholarly endeavors are not confined to traditional platforms; he actively engages with the academic community through social media, where he shares his latest research findings and insights on computational social science and disinformation.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto is involved in several cutting-edge research projects that leverage social media analysis to understand and combat disinformation. His recent work delves into the coordination of media manipulation during significant political events, a testament to his commitment to addressing real-world challenges through rigorous research. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he is a pivotal figure, contributing to the department's growth and the broader academic discourse on digital communication.

Professor Giglietto remains an active voice on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he engages with both the academic and broader communities. His contributions continue to influence the field of Internet Studies, making him a sought-after expert and thought leader in understanding the digital landscape's complexities. His dedication to advancing knowledge and addressing societal issues through research is reflected in his impressive body of work, which continues to inspire and inform scholars and practitioners alike.

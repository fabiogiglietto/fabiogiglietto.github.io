Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound dedication to the exploration of information theory, communication, and society, his work intricately examines the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, with publications in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's research is characterized by a keen focus on the analysis of social media dynamics and the dissemination of disinformation within digital spaces, a topic he extensively explores in his publication "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system." His recent projects delve into the coordination of media manipulation, highlighted by his study on link-sharing behaviors during the Italian elections, which contributes to a broader understanding of the digital information ecosystem.

With a prolific record of over 20 publications and a substantial number of citations, Dr. Giglietto continues to influence the field through his insights on computational social science. His current endeavors include exploring the latest trends in social media analysis, supported by his active engagement on platforms like BlueSky and Threads, where he shares his findings and connects with fellow researchers. His work remains at the forefront of addressing the challenges posed by digital technologies in contemporary society, earning him recognition and numerous citations in academic circles.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Dr. Giglietto is committed to advancing the field through collaborative research projects and contributions to international conferences. His engagement with the academic community, both online and offline, underscores his dedication to fostering a deeper understanding of how digital platforms shape modern communication and societal perceptions. As a thought leader, he continues to contribute valuable perspectives on the evolving landscape of internet studies, making a significant impact on both theoretical frameworks and applied research.

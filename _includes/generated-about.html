Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He specializes in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work has been prominently featured in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's extensive research portfolio includes significant contributions to understanding media manipulation, disinformation, and the dynamics of social media as a research data source. Among his most cited works are "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His scholarly impact is reflected in his total of 1747 citations across 20 publications.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is deeply engaged in projects exploring computational social science and the role of social media in shaping public discourse. His recent work on coordinated link-sharing behavior during Italian elections is a testament to his ongoing commitment to investigating the mechanics of digital communication. Additionally, his active presence on professional networks like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn provides insights into his latest research endeavors and collaborative projects.

Beyond academia, Professor Giglietto contributes to the broader conversation on media literacy and the societal implications of digital technologies. His efforts in these areas highlight his dedication to both advancing academic knowledge and fostering informed public engagement with digital media landscapes.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is renowned for his expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His academic contributions have been widely published in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, reflecting his profound impact on the field.

Professor Giglietto's research primarily delves into the dynamics of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His work, which often explores the participatory aspects of digital platforms, has garnered significant attention, evidenced by his highly cited publications such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His ongoing projects continue to investigate the role of digital technologies in shaping societal narratives and the spread of information across various media.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community and the public through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he shares insights from his current research projects and participates in discussions on contemporary issues related to media manipulation and misinformation. His recent work includes examining coordinated link-sharing behaviors during political events and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems, showcasing his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by digital media in today's information landscape.

With a total of 20 publications and 1,749 citations, Fabio Giglietto remains a pivotal figure in internet studies, contributing to both theoretical advancements and practical solutions in the study of digital media. His dedication to exploring the societal implications of digital technologies continues to inspire students and fellow researchers alike, fostering a deeper understanding of the evolving communication landscape in the digital age.

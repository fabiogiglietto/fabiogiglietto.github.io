Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His scholarly work focuses on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, exploring the dynamic intersection of these fields. As an academic deeply invested in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto has contributed extensively to the academic discourse through publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

With a robust portfolio of research, Professor Giglietto has made significant strides in understanding how digital media and technology influence public discourse and societal structures. His notable publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered considerable citations, reflecting its impact in the field. His research on the manipulation of media narratives during Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems underscores his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in media studies.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects examining the role of social media in the dissemination of disinformation and the implications of computational social science on societal communication patterns. His recent work delves into the role of digital platforms in shaping public opinion, supported by his active presence on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon where he shares insights on his ongoing research. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he continues to mentor students and collaborate with fellow researchers, contributing to the vibrant academic community dedicated to advancing our understanding of digital media's impact on society.

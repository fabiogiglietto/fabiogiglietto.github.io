Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is part of the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. He is a distinguished scholar with a profound interest in the intersections between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto's research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, areas where he has made significant contributions through numerous publications in esteemed journals like the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has been at the forefront of research on social media analysis, disinformation, and the dynamics of digital communication. His widely-cited works, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," underscore his expertise in understanding the complex narratives and behaviors that shape media consumption and misinformation in the digital age. These publications have contributed to his impressive total of 1,751 citations across 20 works.

In addition to his scholarly publications, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the academic community and the public through various platforms. He frequently shares insights on his ongoing projects and recent findings on BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he discusses the implications of coordinated media manipulation and the evolving landscape of digital communication. His recent research projects, often supported by notable grants, continue to explore the boundaries of computational social science, focusing on how digital traces can inform our understanding of societal trends and behaviors.

Professor Giglietto's commitment to advancing the field of Internet Studies is evident not only in his teaching and research but also in his continuous efforts to bridge academia and practical societal applications. His work not only enriches academic discourse but also provides valuable perspectives on the role of digital technologies in contemporary society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey is deeply rooted in examining the nuanced interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a robust focus on the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto has significantly contributed to the field through his extensive research and publications. His work is well-regarded and has been featured in prominent journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has published 20 influential papers, amassing a total of 1,735 citations, reflecting the significant impact of his research on the scholarly community. Some of his most cited works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source". These publications underscore his expertise in social media analysis and the implications of digital platforms on public discourse and media manipulation.

Currently, Professor Giglietto's research continues to explore the dynamics of disinformation and social media, with a keen interest in computational social science methodologies. His recent projects have delved into examining coordinated link sharing behaviors during elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems. Active in academic circles and on various social platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, he engages with peers and the public, sharing insights from his latest research endeavors and contributing to ongoing discussions in the field.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing our understanding of digital communication systems is further reflected in his teaching at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he inspires the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies. His commitment to education and research excellence is paralleled by his active participation in academic conferences and his role in shaping contemporary discourse on media and technology.

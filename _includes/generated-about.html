Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic background, Professor Giglietto is deeply engaged in the exploration of the theory of information, communication, and society. His research primarily focuses on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, offering valuable insights into how these elements converge and interact in contemporary society.

Throughout his scholarly career, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field, publishing extensively in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His notable works include studies on the dynamics of second screens and participation, as well as the impact of social media on political elections, which have collectively garnered over 1,740 citations. His recent research projects continue to delve into pressing issues like disinformation and media manipulation, reflecting his commitment to understanding the evolving digital landscape.

Beyond his academic publications, Professor Giglietto is actively involved in cutting-edge projects related to social media analysis and computational social science. He frequently shares his insights and updates on current projects through professional networks such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he engages with a broader audience on topics like the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse. His engagement in these platforms underscores his dedication to fostering informed discussions about the societal implications of digital technologies.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto continues to inspire students and colleagues alike with his innovative teaching and research. His work not only enhances academic understanding but also contributes to broader societal debates about the influence of digital media in modern life. As an influential figure in the field of Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto remains at the forefront of research, continually exploring new methodologies and perspectives to address the challenges and opportunities presented by the digital age.

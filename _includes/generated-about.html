Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is an integral part of the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a profound interest in the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto explores the theory of information and communication within society. His scholarly contributions have been featured in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, marking him as a leading voice in the field.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the complex dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science. His notable work includes the analysis of coordinated link-sharing behaviors during Italian elections and exploring the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems, which have garnered significant citations and recognition in the academic community. His publication "Second Screen and Participation" has been particularly influential, reflecting his commitment to understanding digital interaction in contemporary media landscapes.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in various research projects and collaborations, often sharing insights and developments through platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn. He is currently investigating the role of social media in shaping public discourse and the implications of digital platforms on democratic processes. His work continues to receive support through grants and collaborations, contributing to advancements in Internet studies and providing valuable perspectives on the challenges and opportunities presented by digital communication technologies.

Through his teaching and research, Fabio Giglietto remains dedicated to advancing knowledge in Internet studies, fostering a deeper understanding of how digital technologies influence society. His expertise and ongoing contributions reflect a commitment to exploring the evolving landscape of media and communication, positioning him as a pivotal figure in the academic study of the Internet and its societal impacts.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, serving within the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. He is renowned for his expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His extensive body of work has contributed significantly to journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the intricacies of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. He has been instrumental in exploring how digital platforms influence public discourse and media manipulation. Among his notable publications, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source" have garnered hundreds of citations, reflecting their impact in the field.

Recently, Fabio has been actively involved in research projects that examine the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems and coordinated behaviors in media manipulation during elections. His work, "It takes a village to manipulate the media," highlights the complexities of information dissemination in the digital age. Engaging with professional communities on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, he continues to share insights and collaborate on projects addressing contemporary challenges in media and communication studies.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto is dedicated to mentoring the next generation of scholars, guiding them through the evolving landscape of internet studies and digital communication. His contributions to academia and society are recognized by peers and students alike, making him a pivotal figure in understanding the role of media and technology in shaping modern society.

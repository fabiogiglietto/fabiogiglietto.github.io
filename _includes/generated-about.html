Fabio Giglietto PhD, is a renowned Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As an Associate Professor, he brings a wealth of knowledge and experience to his role, where he delves deeply into the theory of information, communication, and society. His research is particularly focused on the intricate interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Fabio has made significant contributions to the academic world, evidenced by his extensive publications in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Fabio has authored over 20 scholarly publications, amassing a total of 1747 citations, highlighting the impact of his work on the academic community. Notable among his publications are studies on social media dynamics and the phenomenon of 'fake news,' such as his highly-cited work, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered 397 citations. His research not only advances academic understanding but also offers critical insights into the implications of digital technologies on communication practices.

In recent years, Fabio has continued to explore the realms of social media analysis and disinformation, engaging with the latest developments in computational social science. His involvement in projects analyzing coordinated media manipulation during Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems underscores his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in the digital age. Fabio actively shares his insights and engages with the broader academic community through platforms like BlueSky, where his contributions to discussions on digital media and society are highly regarded.

Fabio's dedication to his field is reflected in his ongoing efforts to push the boundaries of research in Internet Studies. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he not only contributes as a professor but also inspires students and fellow researchers to critically engage with the evolving landscape of digital communication. His work continues to shape the discourse around the societal impacts of media and technology, making him a pivotal figure in the field of Internet Studies.

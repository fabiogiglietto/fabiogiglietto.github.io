Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen focus on the interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto explores the evolving landscape of information, communication, and society. His scholarly work has been widely recognized, with publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication and Information, Communication & Society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field, particularly through his research on social media analysis and disinformation. Among his notable works, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" has garnered extensive citations, reflecting his impact on understanding media consumption patterns. His research continues to delve into the dynamics of coordinated media manipulation, as evidenced by his study on link-sharing behavior during Italian elections.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in examining the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse. His recent projects include analyzing the spread of false information in hybrid news systems and the use of social media as a research data source. Through his work, he aims to uncover the intricate mechanisms that drive online communication and its implications for society. As a thought leader in his field, he frequently shares insights on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, fostering discussions on the future of digital communication.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is dedicated to advancing the academic community's understanding of internet studies. His contributions have not only enriched the curriculum but have also inspired a new generation of researchers to explore the complexities of digital interactions. Whether through his publications, teaching, or online engagement, Fabio Giglietto remains at the forefront of exploring how digital technologies influence modern communication landscapes.

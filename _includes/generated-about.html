Fabio Giglietto is Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Università di Urbino Carlo Bo, where he also earned his doctorate. His research operates at the intersection of computational social science, digital platform analysis, and political communication, with a particular focus on coordinated behavior and information disorders. Within the classroom, he translates these cutting-edge research areas into practice by teaching courses on Generative AI and Media as well as Digital Social Network Analysis. Through these academic and instructional efforts, he examines how digital systems shape public discourse and modern social structures.

Since 2017, he has served as the founder and coordinator of the Mapping Italian News Research Program (MINE). This initiative serves as an institutional umbrella for his research line, having hosted a succession of externally funded sub-projects investigating public opinion, health information, and electoral processes. While these competitive projects have concluded and no external grants are currently active, his recent European engagements include serving as the WP4 Leader for the Horizon Europe project vera.ai, which concluded in 2025, and as a partner on the PROMPT consortium, which wrapped up in early 2026. Both projects focused on identifying and mitigating disinformation narratives across Europe.

As part of his methodological contributions, he co-developed CooRnet, an open-source R package that pioneered the Coordinated Link Sharing Behavior detection method used globally by researchers and fact-checkers. Following the shutdown of Meta's CrowdTangle in August 2024, CooRnet was discontinued at the operational level. However, the core methodology lives on through CooRTweet, an independent, downstream R package maintained by other researchers that continues to detect coordinated social media behavior across modern platforms. Through these methodological innovations, his work has significantly advanced the empirical study of coordinated manipulation online.

Beyond his academic research, he is a founding partner of Digit Srl, a benefit-corporation spin-off of the University of Urbino Carlo Bo that designs digital platforms dedicated to civic participation, social innovation, sustainability, and scientific dissemination. He is also highly active in the international scientific community, holding memberships in the International Communication Association, the Association of Internet Researchers, and the Italian Association for Political Communication. Additionally, he has contributed to the governance of his field by serving on the board of the Research Committee on Sociocybernetics within the International Sociological Association.

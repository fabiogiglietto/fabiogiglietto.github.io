Fabio Giglietto, PhD, holds the esteemed position of Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo's Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a profound dedication to exploring the nexus between social systems, media, and digital technologies, his work significantly advances our understanding of the theory of information, communication, and society. His teaching in Internet Studies underpins a research portfolio that addresses critical questions around the interplay of social media, digital communication, and societal impacts.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions are both prolific and impactful. He has authored numerous articles that have been published in prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media. Notable among his works is the insightful analysis on "Second Screen and Participation," which alone has garnered over 400 citations, underscoring the significance of his research in understanding digital media's role in contemporary society. His investigations into media manipulation, disinformation, and the dynamics of social media discourse, such as through the study of coordinated link-sharing behaviors during Italian elections, highlight his contributions to computational social science and the study of digital cultures.

Engaging actively with the academic and broader community, Giglietto extends his research dialogues through social media platforms, where he discusses recent findings, ongoing projects, and perspectives on current digital communication trends. His recent activities suggest a keen focus on analyzing disinformation and social media's role in shaping public opinion, reflecting his commitment to applying academic insights to real-world challenges. As a leading figure in Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto continues to influence both scholarly and public discourse on the critical issues at the intersection of technology, society, and communication.

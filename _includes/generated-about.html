Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey is marked by a profound exploration of the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Through his pedagogical and research endeavors, Professor Giglietto delves into the theory of information and communication, contributing significantly to the understanding of how these elements shape modern society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly output is robust, with his research widely published in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His seminal works, including the highly cited "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," highlight his analytical prowess in examining social media dynamics and their implications in societal contexts. His research on coordinated link sharing behavior during Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems underscores his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in digital communication.

In recent years, Professor Giglietto has focused on social media analysis and disinformation, leveraging computational social science methods to unravel the complexities of digital interactions. His latest projects continue to push the boundaries of understanding in these areas, reflecting his active engagement with cutting-edge research. Through platforms like LinkedIn and BlueSky, he shares insights into ongoing studies and collaborates with global scholars to foster a deeper comprehension of the digital landscape.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is not only an academic leader but also a mentor, guiding the next generation of scholars in navigating the evolving field of Internet studies. His dedication to advancing knowledge and fostering an informed digital society remains the cornerstone of his professional mission, as evidenced by his prolific publication record and active participation in academic discourse worldwide.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a rich academic background and a keen interest in the intersections of social systems, media, and digital technologies, he has established himself as a thought leader in the field of Internet Studies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, exploring how digital platforms influence contemporary social dynamics.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the academic community, publishing extensively in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Among his notable works is the highly cited paper "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has been referenced 397 times, reflecting its impact on the study of social media and user engagement. His research addresses critical issues such as media manipulation and the dissemination of disinformation, as evidenced by his acclaimed publication on coordinated link-sharing behavior during the Italian elections.

In his current projects, Professor Giglietto continues to explore the dynamics of disinformation and social media analysis, employing computational social science methods to uncover the intricacies of digital communication. Engaging with the academic community through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, he shares insights into his latest research endeavors and collaborations. His work not only advances academic knowledge but also provides valuable perspectives on the role of digital technologies in shaping societal narratives.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto's contributions extend beyond research, as he is dedicated to mentoring the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies. His commitment to fostering an inclusive and dynamic academic environment underscores his role as a leader in the field. Through his ongoing research and engagement with both academic peers and the public, Fabio Giglietto remains at the forefront of understanding and interpreting the complexities of the digital age.

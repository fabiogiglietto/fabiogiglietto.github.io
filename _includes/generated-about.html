Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he holds a prominent position in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. His scholarly endeavors are deeply rooted in the theory of information, communication, and society, particularly examining the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto's extensive body of work is recognized in prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, underscoring his significant contributions to the field.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio is particularly notable for its focus on the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and the computational analysis of digital communication patterns. His acclaimed publication, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has been cited extensively, reflecting its impact in understanding social media's role in public discourse. His work on the manipulation of media and the circulation of 'fake news' within hybrid news systems further demonstrates his critical insights into contemporary media challenges.

Currently engaged in pioneering research projects, Professor Giglietto is exploring the evolving landscape of digital communication and its societal implications. His recent activities, as shared on professional platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, include collaborative projects that harness computational social science methods to tackle issues of misinformation and coordinated link-sharing behaviors during electoral processes. His active engagement in these areas highlights his commitment to advancing knowledge and fostering informed public discourse in the digital age.

With a total of 20 publications and 1,751 citations, Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions continue to shape the discourse on media and technology. His dedication to academic excellence and innovative research ensures his position as a leading voice in the field of Internet Studies, fostering a deeper understanding of the complex interactions between digital platforms and society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen interest in the theory of information, communication, and the intricate relationship between social systems, media, and digital technologies, he has made significant contributions to the field. As an active researcher, Professor Giglietto has published extensively in renowned journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, among others.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio reflects a deep engagement with the complexities of digital communication and its societal implications. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant attention with 397 citations, and "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections," underscoring his expertise in analyzing social media dynamics and disinformation. His recent endeavors continue to explore the intersection of digital platforms and public discourse, contributing to a greater understanding of how false information circulates within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is involved in cutting-edge research on social media analysis and computational social science, focusing on the impact of digital technologies on communication practices. His active presence on social media platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn reflects his commitment to engaging with contemporary debates on digital communication and disinformation. Through his work, he aims to foster a deeper understanding of the evolving digital landscape and its role in shaping public discourse.

In addition to his research, Professor Giglietto is dedicated to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars in the field of Internet Studies. His academic endeavors are supported by various research grants, enabling him to advance his investigations into the complexities of media manipulation and the role of digital platforms in modern society. As an influential figure in his field, Fabio Giglietto continues to contribute to the academic community with his insightful research and thought leadership.

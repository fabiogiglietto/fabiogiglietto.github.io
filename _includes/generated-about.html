Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As a leading figure in the field of digital communication, he focuses on the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His academic pursuits are well-reflected in his extensive body of work, published in prominent journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Prof. Giglietto is renowned for his research on the interplay between digital platforms and societal communication, particularly through the lens of computational social science. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant academic attention with 398 citations. His research endeavors delve deeply into topics such as disinformation and media manipulation, exemplified by his study on coordinated link-sharing behaviors during Italian elections.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Prof. Giglietto is actively involved in pioneering research projects that explore the ever-evolving landscape of social media. His recent analyses of misinformation and discursive strategies on digital platforms contribute significantly to our understanding of contemporary communication phenomena. Through his teachings and scholarly activities, he continues to influence the next generation of internet studies scholars.

Prof. Giglietto's professional engagements extend beyond academia, as he frequently shares insights and developments from his research on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn. His ongoing projects reflect a commitment to addressing the challenges of information dissemination in the digital age, solidifying his reputation as an expert in the field of communication and digital media studies.

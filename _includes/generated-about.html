Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is an integral part of the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a strong foundation in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's work primarily focuses on the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research has been widely recognized, with publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the academic community with his insightful research on topics like social media analysis, disinformation, and coordinated media manipulation. His work, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has garnered substantial attention, emphasizing his expertise in digital media studies. In addition to his published research, he is actively engaged in exploring the evolving landscape of computational social science, often sharing insights on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, where he discusses the impact of digital communication on society.

As an Associate Professor, Fabio Giglietto is deeply involved in teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies. His courses at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo equip students with the critical skills needed to navigate and analyze the digital world. His dedication to education is matched by his commitment to research, as evidenced by his extensive publication record and the numerous citations his work has received, totaling over 1,700.

Professor Giglietto's recent endeavors include leading research projects that investigate the spread of disinformation and the role of social media in contemporary communication. His active engagement with the academic and broader community is reflected in his contributions to recent conferences and collaborative projects, making him a key figure in the ongoing discourse around digital media and society.

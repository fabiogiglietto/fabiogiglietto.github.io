Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound commitment to understanding the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, he has carved a niche in the field of Internet Studies. Professor Giglietto is deeply invested in exploring the theory of information, communication, and society, with his work frequently contributing to prominent journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio is extensive, comprising over 20 publications with a notable citation count exceeding 1700. His work on topics such as coordinated link sharing behavior during Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems has garnered significant academic attention. His influential paper, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," is highly cited and reflects his expertise in analyzing social media dynamics. His current research projects continue to delve into the complexities of disinformation, social media analysis, and computational social science, leveraging platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as critical data sources.

In addition to his scholarly contributions, Professor Giglietto remains actively engaged with the academic community and beyond through his professional activities on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon. His recent projects have focused on the evolving landscape of social media, examining both its potential and limitations as a tool for societal change. As a thought leader, he frequently shares insights on digital media trends and their implications for communication sciences, demonstrating his commitment to bridging academic research with real-world applications.

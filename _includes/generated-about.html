Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. He is particularly interested in the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and the implications of computational social science, which are reflected in his extensive body of work published in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's influential research includes highly cited works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His insightful analyses on media manipulation and the spread of false information, especially during pivotal events like the Italian elections, have garnered significant attention and citations, underscoring his role as a leading voice in Internet Studies.

In addition to his academic contributions, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged with the broader scholarly community through various platforms. His recent posts on BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn reveal an ongoing commitment to advancing research on media manipulation and disinformation. His collaborative projects often receive acclaim, securing grants and awards that support cutting-edge research in computational social science. Through these efforts, Professor Giglietto continues to influence both academic discourse and practical applications in the field of digital communication.

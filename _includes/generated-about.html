Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. Renowned for his research on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto explores the theory of information, communication, and society. His prolific contributions have been published in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, showcasing his expertise and dedication to advancing the field.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the dynamics of digital media, focusing on phenomena like social media analysis and the spread of disinformation. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant academic attention with 397 citations. Furthermore, his exploration of media manipulation during the Italian elections demonstrates his commitment to understanding the societal impacts of coordinated online behaviors. His studies are pivotal in unveiling how false information circulates within the hybrid news system, a topic he has extensively analyzed in his acclaimed publication, “Fake news is the invention of a liar.”

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in research projects that investigate the role of social media platforms in shaping public discourse. His recent activities on academic social networks and professional platforms indicate his ongoing interest in computational social science and the evolution of digital communication strategies. Through his work, he aims to enhance the understanding of how digital technologies influence societal interactions and the dissemination of information.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is not only a dedicated educator, teaching courses in Internet Studies, but also a leader in research initiatives that seek to address contemporary challenges in media and communication. His continuous engagement with the academic community and his presence on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn reflect his commitment to fostering an informed and connected society. With 20 publications and over 1747 citations, Professor Giglietto remains a prominent figure in the field of Internet Studies, contributing valuable insights and fostering meaningful dialogue in the ever-evolving digital landscape.

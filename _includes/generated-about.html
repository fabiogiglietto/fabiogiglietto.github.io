Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He specializes in the intersection of social systems, media, and digital technologies, with a keen focus on the theory of information, communication, and society. As a prolific scholar, Professor Giglietto's research has been featured in leading academic journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the field of Internet Studies with a robust portfolio of publications. His acclaimed work, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has garnered 397 citations, reflecting his influence in the study of digital media participation. His research delves into contemporary issues such as the manipulation of media during elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems, underscoring his commitment to understanding the complexities of digital communication.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader scholarly community through various platforms. He shares insights and developments on social media platforms like BlueSky (@fabiogiglietto.bsky.social) and LinkedIn, where he discusses ongoing projects related to social media analysis and disinformation. His recent endeavors include examining coordinated link-sharing behaviors during elections and exploring the ethical dimensions of using social media as a research data source.

Professor Giglietto's contributions to the field have been recognized with numerous citations, totaling 1,748 across 20 publications. His commitment to advancing Internet Studies is further evidenced by his involvement in cutting-edge research projects and collaborations. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he continues to inspire the next generation of scholars, fostering a deeper understanding of the digital landscapes that shape our world.

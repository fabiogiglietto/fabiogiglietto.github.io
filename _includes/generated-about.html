Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen focus on the intricate interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, he has established himself as a leading figure in the field of communication studies. His research delves deeply into the theory of information, communication, and society, shedding light on the dynamic relationships that define our digital age.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions are well-recognized, with numerous publications in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Some of his most cited works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His publications, totaling 20, have amassed over 1750 citations, reflecting the significant impact of his research on the academic community.

Recently, Professor Giglietto has been actively engaged in research projects that explore the realms of social media analysis and disinformation, contributing to our understanding of how false information circulates within hybrid news systems. He continues to explore coordinated link-sharing behavior during electoral processes, an area that remains crucial in contemporary societal discourse. Through his activity on professional platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, he shares insights from his ongoing projects and discusses the evolving challenges in computational social science.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is not only a dedicated educator but also a mentor to students and young researchers, fostering an environment of critical inquiry and innovation. His commitment to advancing the field of Internet Studies is reflected in his continuous pursuit of knowledge and his active participation in the academic community. As a thought leader, he remains at the forefront of discussions on media manipulation and the digital transformation of society.

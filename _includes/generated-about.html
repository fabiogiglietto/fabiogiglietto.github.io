Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he contributes to the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. His academic journey is characterized by a profound dedication to understanding the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As a leading expert in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto delves into how these elements intersect and influence one another in our increasingly digital age.

Over the years, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field through his extensive research and publications. His works have been featured in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Notable among his publications are research articles like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has been cited 398 times, underscoring its impact in the field. His research frequently explores the phenomena of social media analysis and disinformation, with a keen interest in how these platforms can both inform and mislead public discourse.

Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in several contemporary research projects that reflect his ongoing commitment to the study of digital culture and communication. His recent endeavors include examining the role of coordinated link-sharing behavior during electoral processes and the circulation of misinformation within hybrid news systems. This work not only advances academic understanding but also addresses pressing societal issues surrounding media manipulation and the spread of false information.

In addition to his research, Professor Giglietto maintains an active presence on professional networks such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he shares insights into his ongoing projects and engages with the scholarly community. His current focus includes exploring computational social science methodologies to better understand the societal impacts of social media. Through his teaching and research, Professor Giglietto continues to inspire the next generation of scholars and contribute to the broader understanding of digital interactions in contemporary society.

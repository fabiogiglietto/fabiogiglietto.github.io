Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As an Associate Professor, he delves into the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research primarily focuses on the theory of information and communication, exploring how these elements shape society. With a prolific publication record, his work has been featured in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the academic community, authoring over 20 publications with a total of 1747 citations. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," reflecting his keen interest in the evolving landscape of digital media. Recently, his research has expanded to analyze coordinated link-sharing behaviors during political events and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is involved in cutting-edge research projects that explore the impact of social media platforms on public discourse and the spread of disinformation. His active engagement on professional platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon highlights his ongoing commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by computational social science and digital communication. Through his work, he aims to contribute to the development of strategies that mitigate the effects of misinformation and enhance the reliability of digital information ecosystems.

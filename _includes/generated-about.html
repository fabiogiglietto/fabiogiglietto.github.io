Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic foundation and a passion for exploring the intersections of social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has established himself as a leading figure in the field of Internet Studies. His teaching and research focus on the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has published extensively on these topics in respected journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, Social Media + Society, and Social Science Computer Review.

Dr. Giglietto's prolific research output includes notable works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant citations, reflecting its impact in the academic community. His work delves into the dynamics of social media platforms, exploring issues such as disinformation and coordinated link-sharing behavior, particularly during critical events like the 2018 and 2019 Italian elections. His publication "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system" exemplifies his commitment to understanding the complexities of information dissemination in the digital age.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Dr. Giglietto is actively engaged in current research on social media analysis and computational social science, contributing to the broader discourse on media manipulation and the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse. His recent activities on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon highlight his ongoing projects and collaborations that aim to address pressing issues in digital communication and information theory.

With a total of 20 publications and over 1,700 citations, Dr. Giglietto continues to influence the field through his innovative research and thought leadership. His ongoing investigations into the use of social media as a research data source and the potential of these platforms to both inform and mislead underscore his commitment to advancing our understanding of the digital world. Dr. Giglietto remains a pivotal figure at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he not only inspires students through teaching but also drives forward critical research that addresses contemporary challenges in communication sciences.

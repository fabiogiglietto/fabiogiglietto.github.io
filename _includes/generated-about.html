Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a research focus that intricately weaves together the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto explores the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions are widely recognized, with his work appearing in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research offers profound insights into how digital platforms influence public discourse and societal perceptions. His notable publications include influential studies on "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," which have collectively garnered significant academic attention with over 1,700 citations. In recent years, his research has delved into topics like media manipulation and the circulation of misinformation within hybrid news systems, reflecting his commitment to understanding and mitigating the impact of disinformation in digital media landscapes.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that leverage computational social science methods to analyze social media dynamics and misinformation. His work often intersects with current events, such as analyzing coordinated link-sharing behaviors during elections, reflecting his dedication to applying research to real-world challenges. Through his presence on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, he shares insights and fosters discussions around these critical issues, demonstrating his role as both a researcher and a public intellectual.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto contributes not only through his research but also through his teaching, where he guides the next generation of scholars in navigating the complexities of the digital age. His commitment to education and research excellence continues to position him as a leading figure in the field of Internet Studies, both in Italy and internationally.

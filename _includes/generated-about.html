Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a comprehensive background in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research primarily explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in his field, contributing extensively to renowned journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

His scholarly work includes influential studies such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has received over 400 citations, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His research on digital media manipulation, particularly during pivotal events such as the Italian elections, underscores his commitment to understanding the dynamics of misinformation in the hybrid news system. This work is reflected in publications like "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections."

Currently, Professor Giglietto is spearheading projects that delve into the analysis of social media platforms, focusing on the dissemination of disinformation and the role of computational social science in addressing these challenges. He actively engages with the academic and professional community through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he shares insights and developments in his ongoing research. His dedication to advancing the field is further exemplified by his involvement in recent academic collaborations and his continuous contributions to scholarly discourse.

Beyond his research, Professor Giglietto is deeply committed to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies. His courses at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo equip students with critical skills to navigate and analyze the rapidly evolving digital landscape. Through his work, Fabio Giglietto continues to shape our understanding of the digital world and its impact on society.

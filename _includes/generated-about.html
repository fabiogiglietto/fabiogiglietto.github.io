Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is a leading academic in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. His teaching and research are centered on the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a profound interest in the theory of information and communication, Professor Giglietto has been at the forefront of exploring how these elements shape and are shaped by society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field through his extensive publications in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society. His research often delves into the phenomena surrounding media manipulation and disinformation, as evidenced by his highly cited works like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system." His recent projects continue to explore these themes, utilizing platforms like Facebook and Twitter as data sources to understand the spread and impact of false information.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader academic community through various professional activities. He frequently shares insights from his ongoing research on social media analysis and computational social science on platforms like LinkedIn and BlueSky, where his handle is @fabiogiglietto.bsky.social. His current work includes examining coordinated link-sharing behaviors in the context of political elections, further cementing his role as a thought leader in understanding digital communication's societal impacts. Through his innovative research and active engagement, Fabio Giglietto is advancing critical discussions on the role of digital technologies in modern society.

Dr. Fabio Giglietto is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep commitment to exploring the intersections of social systems, media, and digital technologies, he has established himself as a leading voice in the theory of information and communication. His academic journey is marked by a prolific output of research published in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, highlighting his authority in the field.

Dr. Giglietto's research portfolio is extensive, with significant contributions like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered widespread recognition with 398 citations. His scholarly work delves into critical issues such as media manipulation, disinformation, and the impact of digital technologies on society. Notable publications include "It takes a village to manipulate the media" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar," both of which examine the complexities of information dissemination in contemporary media landscapes.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Dr. Giglietto is actively engaged in ongoing research projects that address the challenges posed by disinformation and the role of social media in public discourse. His recent work reflects a keen interest in computational social science, leveraging platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon to share insights and collaborate with peers. As an Associate Professor, he continues to mentor emerging scholars and contribute to the academic community through dynamic research and thought leadership at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo.

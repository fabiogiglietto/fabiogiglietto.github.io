Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As an Associate Professor, he passionately explores the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly work predominantly focuses on the theory of information, communication, and society, contributing significantly to the academic discourse through numerous publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's prolific research includes groundbreaking studies like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered considerable attention with 397 citations. His investigative work on the role of digital platforms as research data sources and the manipulation of media during elections highlights his commitment to understanding the societal impacts of digital communication. His research endeavors are further exemplified by his recent work on disinformation and computational social science, which continue to shape contemporary understanding in these fields.

Beyond his publications, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the academic and broader community through various platforms, including BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he shares insights into his ongoing projects and research initiatives. His recent activities reveal a keen interest in the application of social media analysis tools to address issues related to misinformation and media manipulation. His contributions to these areas have not only advanced academic knowledge but also informed public discourse and policy-making.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto's work is integral to the Department of Communication Sciences, where he inspires students and fellow researchers alike. His dedication to excellence in research and teaching makes him a pivotal figure in the field of Internet Studies, continuously advancing the understanding of how digital technologies intersect with societal structures. As an influential voice in the academic community, Professor Giglietto's work remains at the forefront of exploring the complexities of our digital age.

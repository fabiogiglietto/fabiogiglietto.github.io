Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated passion for understanding the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has dedicated his career to exploring the theory of information, communication, and society. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, with numerous publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's research is at the forefront of examining the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science. His work often delves into how digital platforms influence public discourse and behavior, a theme evident in his widely cited publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system." Currently, he is actively involved in projects analyzing the impact of coordinated link-sharing behaviors during political events, further cementing his role as a thought leader in the field.

Beyond academia, Dr. Giglietto frequently shares insights on emerging trends in media and technology on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, engaging with a global audience. His recent endeavors include collaborative research initiatives aimed at developing innovative methodologies for social media analysis, supported by significant grants and institutional collaborations. Through his teaching and research, Dr. Giglietto continues to inspire the next generation of scholars while contributing to a deeper understanding of the digital age.

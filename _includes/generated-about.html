Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's research delves into the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions are widely recognized, with publications featured in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's academic journey is marked by a prolific output of research, including notable works like "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," which have significantly contributed to the discourse on social media and communication dynamics. His work has garnered significant academic attention, accumulating a total of 1,748 citations across 20 publications, underscoring his impact in the field of Internet studies. His recent research continues to explore critical issues in social media analysis and disinformation, reflecting his ongoing commitment to advancing understanding in these areas.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is actively involved in teaching Internet Studies, where he imparts his extensive knowledge and research insights to students. His current projects focus on the evolving landscape of digital media and its societal implications, as evidenced by his recent activity on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he engages with contemporary debates and shares his latest research findings. Additionally, his work on computational social science is paving the way for innovative approaches to studying digital communication phenomena.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto is frequently involved in professional activities that include speaking engagements and collaborations with fellow researchers. His contributions to the field have not only enriched academic discourse but have also provided critical insights into the functioning of the hybrid news system and the spread of misinformation. Through his dedication and scholarly excellence, Fabio Giglietto continues to shape the future of Internet Studies, making significant strides in understanding the digital world's complexities.

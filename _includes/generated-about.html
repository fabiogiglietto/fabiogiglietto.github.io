Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he also teaches Internet Studies. His scholarly work focuses on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. An esteemed researcher, Dr. Giglietto has published extensively in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, contributing significantly to the fields of information theory, communication, and social science.

Dr. Giglietto's research interests are centered around social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has been cited 397 times, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," with 276 citations. His insights into media manipulation, particularly during political events, are evident in his analysis of coordinated link-sharing behaviors during the Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Dr. Giglietto is actively engaged in current research projects that explore the dynamics of social media platforms and their impact on public discourse. He frequently shares insights from his latest studies on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he discusses topics such as digital communication strategies and the role of algorithms in shaping media landscapes. His ongoing work continues to shed light on the evolving nature of digital interactions and their societal implications.

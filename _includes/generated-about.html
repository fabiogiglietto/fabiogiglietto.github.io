Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research primarily explores the dynamic relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As a prolific author, Professor Giglietto has contributed extensively to leading academic journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly work is marked by significant contributions to the field, including his highly cited studies such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." These works, alongside others, have collectively garnered over 1,748 citations, underscoring his influence in the domain of digital communication studies.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is deeply engaged in research that explores the phenomena of disinformation and the computational analysis of social media. His recent projects focus on understanding coordinated link sharing behavior and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems. His robust research agenda is further enriched by his participation in international collaborations and conferences, where he frequently shares insights on the interplay between digital media and society.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto remains active in the digital space, sharing his expertise and engaging with scholarly communities on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn. His latest endeavors continue to shape the discourse on media manipulation and social media's impact on public perception, reflecting his dedication to advancing the field of Internet Studies.

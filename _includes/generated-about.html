Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. In his role, he delves into the intricate theory of information, communication, and society, focusing particularly on the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a robust presence in academic circles, Professor Giglietto has contributed extensively to top-tier journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio is expansive and influential, with notable works such as "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," reflecting his keen insight into media interactions and technology's role in society. His investigation into media manipulation during elections and the impact of false information within hybrid news systems adds to his reputation as a leading scholar in the field of digital communication. His publications, accumulating over 1,700 citations, underscore his significant impact and thought leadership.

In recent endeavors, Professor Giglietto continues to explore the complexities of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His active engagement on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon reveals his commitment to advancing understanding in these areas. Current projects, driven by the latest digital trends and challenges, leverage his expertise to address pressing issues in information dissemination and societal impact.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto not only contributes through teaching but also through mentorship and collaborative research initiatives. His work, recognized through various grants and professional accolades, continues to shape the landscape of Internet Studies, empowering future scholars and practitioners in navigating the digital age's challenges and opportunities.

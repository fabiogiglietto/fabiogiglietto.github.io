Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research primarily explores the complex interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto has made substantial contributions to the field, publishing extensively in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has focused on the analysis of social media dynamics, disinformation, and the impact of digital platforms on public discourse. His notable works include research on the participatory role of second screens, the challenges of using social media platforms as research data sources, and the mechanisms of false information propagation within hybrid news systems. His publication, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has garnered significant attention with 397 citations, reflecting its impact on the field.

In recent years, Professor Giglietto has been actively involved in projects addressing the manipulation of media during political events, such as the coordinated link-sharing behavior during the 2018 and 2019 Italian elections. His scholarly work continues to shed light on the strategic use of hashtags for discourse, exemplified by his analysis of #JeNeSuisPasCharlie following the Charlie Hebdo shooting. With a total of 20 publications and 1759 citations, his research not only advances academic understanding but also informs public policy and media practices.

Professor Giglietto remains committed to exploring the evolving landscape of digital communication, frequently contributing to discussions on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon. His current endeavors include examining the influence of social media on societal perceptions and the role of digital literacy in combating disinformation. As a thought leader in his field, he continues to inspire and educate through both his research and teaching at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo.

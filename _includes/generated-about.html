Fabio Giglietto, PhD, serves as a Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is highly regarded for his extensive research on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, which is evident in his numerous publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Fabio's work centers on understanding the dynamics of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on how these elements interact within the digital landscape.

With a robust portfolio of scholarly articles, including highly cited works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," Fabio has contributed significantly to the field of Internet Studies. His research explores the intricacies of digital communication, evident in his analysis of coordinated link-sharing behavior during the Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Fabio is engaged in projects that delve into the computational analysis of social media platforms, examining issues such as disinformation and media manipulation. His ongoing research efforts are supported by active collaborations with international scholars and participation in cutting-edge digital communication networks. Fabio stays connected with the academic community through platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he shares insights from his latest studies and contributes to discussions on the evolving digital media landscape.

Fabio's dedication to the advancement of Internet Studies is reflected in his commitment to teaching and mentorship at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he inspires the next generation of scholars. As a thought leader in his field, Fabio Giglietto continues to shape the discourse around media, technology, and society with his innovative research and scholarly contributions.

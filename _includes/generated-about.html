Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has dedicated his career to exploring the theory of information, communication, and society. His extensive research is published in leading academic journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, showcasing his significant contributions to the field.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio is marked by impactful studies such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have collectively garnered over 1740 citations. His recent work continues to delve into pressing issues such as disinformation and coordinated media manipulation, as evidenced by his study on Italian elections titled "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections."

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research on social media analysis and computational social science, often sharing insights and updates on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon. His work is consistently supported by prestigious grants, reflecting the academic community's recognition of his expertise and the relevance of his research. As an Associate Professor, he remains dedicated to fostering an environment of innovation and critical inquiry at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, guiding students and collaborating with peers on transformative projects that address the evolving challenges of the digital age.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is renowned for his pioneering research in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular emphasis on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His extensive body of work includes publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, reflecting his profound impact on the field.

Professor Giglietto's recent research delves into the dynamics of social media analysis and disinformation, exploring the intricate ways in which information circulates within the hybrid news system. His highly cited works, including studies on coordinated link sharing during Italian elections and the circulation of false information, underscore his commitment to understanding the complexities of digital media landscapes. These contributions have garnered significant attention, with his publications collectively cited over 1,700 times, demonstrating his influence and thought leadership.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he shares insights on his latest research projects and engages in discourse on computational social science. His current endeavors include examining the role of social media in shaping public discourse and the potential of these platforms as research data sources. Through his innovative work, Professor Giglietto continues to push the boundaries of Internet Studies, contributing valuable perspectives to the ongoing dialogue on media and society.

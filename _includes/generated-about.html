Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic background and a passion for exploring the intersections of social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field of Internet Studies. He is renowned for his comprehensive research on the theory of information, communication, and society, focusing on the dynamic relationships between media and digital technologies.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly work has been published in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His influential studies, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," have garnered widespread recognition and citations, reflecting his expertise in social media analysis and the dissemination of information.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research projects that analyze the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse, particularly focusing on disinformation and the manipulation of media during electoral processes. His recent work, "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections," highlights his commitment to understanding the complexities of media ecosystems in contemporary society. Through his active presence on professional networks like BlueSky and Mastodon, Professor Giglietto continues to share insights and collaborate with fellow researchers and practitioners in the field.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is dedicated to fostering a vibrant academic environment that encourages innovation and critical thinking. His teaching and mentorship have inspired a new generation of scholars in Internet Studies, contributing to the advancement of knowledge in this rapidly evolving discipline. As an Associate Professor, he remains at the forefront of research in social media analysis and computational social science, actively pushing the boundaries of how digital technologies influence communication and society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor in Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo’s Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a deep-seated passion for exploring the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has emerged as a leading voice in the theory of information, communication, and society. His notable contributions to the field have been published in prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, among others.

Professor Giglietto’s research delves into the multifaceted world of social media analysis and the pervasive issue of disinformation, reflecting his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by the digital landscape. His recent endeavors include examining coordinated link-sharing behaviors during Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems. These studies underscore his dedication to uncovering the complexities of media manipulation and its societal implications.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the academic community through platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he shares insights into his ongoing research projects. His influential work, such as "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," has garnered significant attention, evidenced by numerous citations and widespread recognition. With a total of 20 publications and 1747 citations, his work continues to shape contemporary understanding of digital media interactions.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto not only imparts knowledge through his teaching but also mentors the next generation of scholars, fostering a collaborative environment that encourages critical thinking and innovation. His dedication to advancing the field of Internet Studies is matched by his enthusiasm for nurturing an academic community that thrives on research excellence and intellectual curiosity.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic endeavors focus on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As an expert in information theory, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field through his extensive research and publications in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio includes influential works like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered over 400 citations. His scholarly interests extend to the study of disinformation and the dynamics of media manipulation, evident in his research on coordinated link-sharing behavior during the Italian elections and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems. His work not only contributes to academic discourse but also informs public understanding of contemporary media phenomena.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research projects that explore social media analysis and the impact of digital platforms on public discourse. His presence on professional networks such as BlueSky and Mastodon provides insights into his ongoing projects and collaborations, reflecting his commitment to advancing the field of computational social science. Through these platforms, he shares his latest findings and engages with a global community of scholars and practitioners.

With a total of 20 publications and 1766 citations, Professor Giglietto's academic influence is both extensive and profound. His dedication to exploring the nuances of digital communication and his role as an educator at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo position him as a leading figure in Internet Studies, continually pushing the boundaries of how digital technologies shape our world.

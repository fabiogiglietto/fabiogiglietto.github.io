Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is an esteemed scholar in the field of Internet Studies, focusing on the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has contributed significantly to the academic community through his extensive publications in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto has authored numerous influential publications, including his widely cited works, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." With a total of 20 publications and over 1,700 citations, his research has profoundly impacted the study of social media analysis and digital communication. His recent projects explore the spread of disinformation and the manipulation of media, especially in the context of political events, as evidenced by his work on coordinated link sharing behavior during the Italian elections.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Dr. Giglietto actively engages with the wider scholarly and professional community. His recent activities on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn reveal his ongoing interest in computational social science and the challenges of managing disinformation in digital spaces. He collaborates with international scholars and participates in various research initiatives, underscoring his commitment to advancing the understanding of digital media's role in contemporary society.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Dr. Giglietto is recognized for his contributions to developing curricula that address the evolving landscape of internet studies. His courses provide students with critical insights into the interplay between technology and society, preparing them to navigate and influence the digital world effectively. Through his teaching and research, he continues to shape the discourse on digital communication and its societal implications.

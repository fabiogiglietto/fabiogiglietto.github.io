Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo's Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. Renowned for his expertise in information theory and communication, Professor Giglietto's research delves into the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, with publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's extensive body of work includes notable publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have collectively garnered over 1,762 citations. His research has significantly advanced the understanding of digital media's role in society, particularly concerning social media analysis and the spread of disinformation.

In his current projects, Professor Giglietto focuses on computational social science, exploring how coordinated link-sharing behaviors influence media narratives, as seen in his recent work on the Italian elections. He remains active in the academic community, contributing insights on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he engages with contemporary discussions on digital communication trends and challenges. His commitment to advancing the field is further evidenced by his involvement in various international research collaborations and his contributions to discussions on the ethical implications of digital media research.

A dedicated educator, Professor Giglietto is passionate about teaching Internet Studies, where he inspires students to critically analyze the evolving digital landscape. His dedication to fostering academic growth and innovation continues to make a significant impact on both his students and the broader academic community.

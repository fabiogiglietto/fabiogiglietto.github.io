Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is recognized for his extensive research on the intricate interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His academic pursuits primarily revolve around the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on how these elements interact in contemporary digital environments. Professor Giglietto is a prolific author, contributing significantly to leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions include notable publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have collectively garnered over 1,740 citations. His research on disinformation and media manipulation during elections, as well as the circulation of 'fake news,' has provided valuable insights into the dynamics of digital communication. His recent work continues to explore the effects of coordinated link sharing behavior on public discourse, reflecting his ongoing commitment to understanding and mitigating the impacts of disinformation in the digital age.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader academic and professional community through platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn. Here, he shares insights related to his current projects, which often involve social media analysis and computational social science. His involvement in these areas not only highlights his dedication to advancing the field but also underscores his role as a thought leader in the study of digital communication.

Professor Giglietto's work continues to influence the academic discourse on media and technology, positioning him as a central figure in the field of Internet Studies. His contributions are not only recognized through his publications but also through his active participation in ongoing discussions about the future of digital communication and its societal implications. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he remains committed to fostering a deeper understanding of the digital world through both his research and teaching.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he contributes to the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a specialization in Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto explores the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have significantly advanced the field, with his work featured in leading academic journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research primarily focuses on the theory of information and communication, and he has a keen interest in understanding how digital technologies shape societal interactions. Notably, his studies on social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube have highlighted both the potential and the challenges these platforms present as sources of research data. His publication, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," is highly cited and underscores his expertise in analyzing online participation trends.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in groundbreaking research that examines the dissemination of disinformation and the role of computational social science in analyzing social media behaviors. His recent projects include an analysis of coordinated link-sharing behavior during the Italian elections, revealing insights into media manipulation strategies. In recognition of his work, he actively shares his findings and insights on platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he connects with fellow researchers and the broader community interested in digital communication phenomena.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has amassed a significant body of work, comprising 20 publications with over 1,700 citations, reflecting the impact and relevance of his research. His dedication to advancing our understanding of digital communication continues to inspire both his students and colleagues, making him a pivotal figure in the evolving landscape of Internet Studies.

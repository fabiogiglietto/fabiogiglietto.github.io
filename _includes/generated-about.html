Fabio Giglietto, PhD, serves as Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a distinguished career dedicated to exploring the intricate dynamics between information theory, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto specializes in examining the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His expertise not only enriches his teaching in Internet Studies but also guides his prolific research endeavors.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions are extensive, with publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, among others. His research, which has garnered over 1800 citations, spans critical issues from second-screen usage and its impact on participation to the manipulation of the media through coordinated link-sharing behaviors. Noteworthy publications include his analysis of the #JeNeSuisPasCharlie discourse and the exploration of 'fake news' within the hybrid news system, reflecting his keen interest in the intersection of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science.

In recent years, Professor Giglietto has continued to engage deeply with current trends and challenges in digital communication. His active participation in professional networks, such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, reveals ongoing projects that focus on the latest in social media analysis and the fight against disinformation online. This engagement not only demonstrates his commitment to applying academic insights to real-world problems but also highlights his role as a thought leader in the evolving field of Internet Studies. Through his innovative research and commitment to excellence in teaching, Professor Giglietto continues to contribute significantly to our understanding of the digital society and its complexities.

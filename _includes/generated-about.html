Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. Known for his extensive research in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto focuses on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work is widely recognized, with publications featured in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has explored the dynamic interplay between media and society, publishing influential research on topics including media manipulation during political events and the spread of disinformation. His acclaimed studies, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections," have collectively garnered over 1,700 citations, underscoring his impact in the field.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto remains actively engaged in the latest developments in social media analysis and computational social science. His recent projects delve into understanding disinformation dynamics and the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse. As an active participant in academic and professional communities, he frequently shares insights and updates on platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, contributing to ongoing discussions about the evolving digital landscape.

Professor Giglietto's contributions to Internet Studies not only enhance academic understanding but also provide valuable insights into the societal implications of digital technologies. His dedication to exploring the complexities of media and communication continues to inspire both his students and the broader academic community.

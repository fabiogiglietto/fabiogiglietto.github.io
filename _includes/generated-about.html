Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is renowned for his expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, with publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant strides in understanding the dynamics of digital communication, particularly through his research on social media analysis and disinformation. His influential works, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," have collectively garnered over 1700 citations, reflecting his impact on the field. His recent projects delve into the coordinated behaviors in media manipulation and the dissemination of false information within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research on computational social science, frequently sharing insights and updates on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon. His commitment to advancing the understanding of digital ecosystems extends beyond academia, as he actively participates in professional activities and collaborations aimed at addressing the challenges posed by misinformation in media. Through his work, he continues to shape the discourse on digital communication, contributing to both theoretical advancements and practical solutions in the field.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a distinguished career in the field of Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto teaches and conducts research on the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly work primarily focuses on the theory of information, communication, and society, with a keen interest in how these elements converge and influence each other.

Professor Giglietto has an extensive publication record, showcasing his expertise in analyzing social media and its impact on communication. His acclaimed publications have appeared in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, Social Media + Society, and Social Science Computer Review. Noteworthy among his works is the article "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant citations, reflecting the impact of his research on the academic community.

Among his recent research endeavors, Professor Giglietto is actively exploring the role of social media in the dissemination of disinformation and the manipulation of media narratives, a topic of pressing global importance. His work on coordinated link sharing behavior during the 2018 and 2019 Italian elections is a testament to his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in media studies. His insights frequently highlight the intricate ways in which false information circulates within the hybrid news system, a subject he explores in his widely cited publication on the topic.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community and the public through various social media platforms, sharing insights and updates on his ongoing projects. His recent activity underscores his dedication to fostering a deeper understanding of digital communication and its societal implications. Professor Giglietto continues to contribute to the advancement of Internet Studies, making significant strides in both research and education at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo.

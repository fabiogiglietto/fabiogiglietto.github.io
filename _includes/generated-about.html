Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a renowned Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound commitment to understanding the complex interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, his scholarly work has significantly contributed to the fields of information and communication theory. Fabio's extensive research addresses the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science, reflecting his dedication to exploring how digital platforms shape contemporary society.

Fabio has authored numerous influential publications, with his work appearing in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His highly-cited studies, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," underscore his expertise in analyzing digital platforms as both social phenomena and research tools. His research on coordinated media manipulation during elections and the spread of false information within hybrid news systems has provided critical insights into the challenges posed by modern media landscapes.

Currently, Fabio is deeply engaged in examining the implications of social media on public discourse and the dissemination of information. His recent projects focus on the role of digital technologies in shaping political communication and public opinion. Through his active presence on platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, he shares insights and engages with a broad community of scholars and practitioners, fostering dialogue on the impact of digital media on society.

As a leading figure in Internet Studies, Fabio Giglietto continues to push the boundaries of research in digital communication. His work not only advances academic understanding but also provides valuable perspectives on the ethical and societal implications of emerging media technologies. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he inspires both students and colleagues with his dedication to exploring the transformative power of the internet in our daily lives.

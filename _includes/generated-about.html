Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his research intricately explores the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Professor Giglietto has significantly contributed to academia through his extensive publications in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has focused his research on social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His work, including notable publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has been widely cited for its insightful analysis of media manipulation and information dissemination within hybrid news systems. His current projects continue to delve into the complexities of coordinated link-sharing behavior and the circulation of false information, especially during pivotal events like elections.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community through platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, where he shares insights on his latest research endeavors and collaborations. His recent activities reflect a commitment to advancing understanding in the field of Internet studies, particularly in the context of emerging digital phenomena and their societal implications. As an Associate Professor at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he remains dedicated to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars in communication sciences.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he also teaches Internet Studies. His academic journey and professional career have been dedicated to exploring the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Fabio's research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has contributed significantly to the field through an array of published works in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his career, Fabio has been at the forefront of examining the dynamic interplay between digital platforms and societal behaviors. His notable publications, including "Second Screen and Participation" and "The open laboratory," have garnered widespread recognition and citations. His research on disinformation and the hybrid news system, exemplified by works such as "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar," continues to inform current debates on media manipulation and the role of social media in shaping public discourse.

Fabio's recent projects focus on computational social science approaches to analyzing social media dynamics, particularly concerning disinformation and coordinated behaviors during critical events like elections. His active engagement on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon highlights his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in media and communication. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Fabio continues to lead innovative research initiatives, contributing to the advancement of knowledge in Internet Studies and fostering a deeper understanding of digital society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, serves as an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound commitment to understanding the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has established himself as a leading scholar in the field of Internet Studies. His research primarily explores the theory of information, communication, and society, with a keen focus on the interplay between social media platforms and societal phenomena.

Professor Giglietto's prolific contributions to the academic community are evidenced by his extensive publications in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Among his notable works, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" has garnered significant attention, reflecting his expertise in analyzing digital communication patterns. His innovative research on the coordinated behaviors observed during electoral processes, particularly in Italy, underscores the critical role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader scholarly community through various professional activities and collaborations. His recent projects delve into the analysis of disinformation and the role of computational social science in addressing contemporary media challenges. As a thought leader in his field, he frequently shares insights on platforms like BlueSky, where he discusses ongoing research and current trends in social media analysis.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing the understanding of digital media's impact on society is further supported by his involvement in several cutting-edge research initiatives at the University of Urbino. His work not only contributes to the academic discourse but also informs policy and practice, highlighting the significance of interdisciplinary approaches in navigating the complexities of the digital age.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, serves as an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto explores the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His extensive research has been published in renowned journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, reflecting his profound impact on the field.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly contributions include pivotal works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant attention with 397 citations. His research on the use of social media platforms as data sources and the manipulation of media during political events has been influential, with significant publications like "The open laboratory" and "It takes a village to manipulate the media." His total of 20 publications and 1740 citations underscore his role as a thought leader in Internet Studies.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research on social media analysis and disinformation, with a particular focus on computational social science. His recent projects include examining the dissemination of false information within digital ecosystems, a topic that resonates with ongoing global conversations about media integrity and digital literacy. His active presence on platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and Threads highlights his commitment to engaging with both academic and public audiences, fostering dialogues about the societal implications of digital technologies.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to his field is further demonstrated by his involvement in recent grants and professional activities that support his research endeavors. His contributions not only advance academic understanding but also inform public policy and media practices. As an educator and researcher, he continues to inspire future scholars in Internet Studies, while his efforts contribute to shaping a more informed and connected society.

Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is a leading figure in Internet Studies, serving as a Professor at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's work explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been featured in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, highlighting his expertise in understanding the dynamics of social media and its impact on society.

Professor Giglietto's notable research includes pioneering studies on media manipulation and disinformation, particularly during significant political events like the Italian elections. His publication, "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections," reflects his ongoing commitment to uncovering the complexities of information dissemination in the digital age. As an active voice in the academic community, he has accumulated a total of 20 publications with over 1,763 citations, underscoring his influence and the relevance of his research.

Beyond his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto is deeply engaged with the broader conversation on digital communication and its societal implications. His presence on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn provides insights into his current projects, which focus on social media analysis and computational social science. These endeavors are supported by recent grants and collaborations that further his investigations into the flow of information in hybrid news systems. Through his work, Professor Giglietto continues to contribute significantly to the discourse on how digital technologies shape contemporary communication landscapes.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As a faculty member of the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies, he specializes in the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a focus on the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's work delves into how digital platforms influence societal structures and behaviors. His research has been featured in leading academic journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto is widely recognized for his contributions to the field, with a robust portfolio of over 20 publications and a total citation count exceeding 1,700. His influential works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source" have significantly impacted our understanding of digital communication. His recent projects continue to explore the complexities of social media analysis and the spread of disinformation, reflecting his commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in the digital age.

Beyond his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader scholarly community through various platforms, including BlueSky (@fabiogiglietto.bsky.social) and LinkedIn. He frequently shares insights from his ongoing research on computational social science and collaborative projects, fostering an inclusive dialogue around the societal implications of digital media. His recent activities also highlight his involvement in interdisciplinary initiatives aiming to mitigate the effects of misinformation and improve digital literacy.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing the field of Internet Studies is further demonstrated by his participation in numerous professional activities and collaborations. His work not only contributes to academic discourse but also informs policy and practice, making a substantial impact on both scholarly and public understanding of digital media's role in contemporary society.

Dr. Fabio Giglietto is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated passion for exploring the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in the field of Internet Studies. His teaching and research focus primarily on the theory of information, communication, and society, areas in which he has contributed significantly through numerous publications in renowned journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's scholarly work is well-recognized, with notable publications including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered 397 citations, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," cited 276 times. His research often delves into the evolving landscape of social media and the dissemination of information, as seen in his study on coordinated link-sharing behavior during Italian elections and his insightful analysis of false information circulation within the hybrid news system. These contributions have solidified his reputation as a thought leader in understanding digital communication's societal impacts.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is actively involved in several innovative research projects that investigate the role of social media in shaping public discourse and the spread of disinformation. He is particularly interested in computational social science methodologies, which allow for a nuanced analysis of large datasets derived from popular platforms such as Twitter and Facebook. His recent engagements and professional activities, highlighted on his social media profiles including BlueSky and LinkedIn, reflect his commitment to advancing the academic conversation around these critical issues.

In addition to his research endeavors, Dr. Giglietto is committed to fostering a collaborative academic environment at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He mentors students and collaborates with fellow researchers, contributing to a vibrant scholarly community. Through his work, Dr. Giglietto continues to influence the field of Internet Studies and inspire the next generation of researchers dedicated to understanding the complex interplay between digital technologies and society.

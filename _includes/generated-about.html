Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a strong academic foundation and an innovative approach to teaching, he imparts knowledge on Internet Studies, focusing on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has made significant contributions to the field through his extensive publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto is renowned for his work on social media analysis and disinformation. His recent research projects explore the dynamics of misinformation in digital environments, particularly focusing on the hybrid news systems and coordinated link-sharing behaviors during political events. Notable among his publications are "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," which have collectively garnered significant citations, reflecting the impact of his work on the academic community.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto engages with the broader community through social media platforms, where he actively discusses his ongoing research and insights into computational social science. His presence on platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn provides a window into his current projects and collaborations. As a thought leader in the analysis of digital communication, he continues to explore the limits and possibilities of using social media as a research data source, contributing to the evolving discourse around digital society.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing the understanding of digital media is further demonstrated by his involvement in international research collaborations and conferences. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he fosters an environment of innovation and inquiry, inspiring the next generation of scholars to explore the transformative power of digital communication. With over 1740 total citations across 20 publications, his work stands as a testament to his expertise and influence in the field of Internet Studies.

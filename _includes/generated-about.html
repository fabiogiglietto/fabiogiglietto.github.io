Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey is marked by a profound exploration of the theory of information, communication, and society, with a keen focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Through his extensive research, Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the understanding of these dynamics, with his work being featured in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's current research delves into the realms of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. He is actively engaged in projects that aim to unravel the complexities of digital communication and its societal implications. His recent endeavors highlight the critical examination of media influence and the proliferation of misinformation in digital spaces, reflecting his commitment to advancing knowledge in these pivotal areas.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto is actively involved in the scholarly community, contributing to various professional activities and discussions. His insights are frequently shared across platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, where he engages with an audience keen on understanding the evolving landscape of internet studies. Through teaching and mentorship, he inspires a new generation of scholars to critically engage with the digital world, fostering an environment of innovation and discovery at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo.

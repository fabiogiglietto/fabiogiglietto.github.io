Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he serves in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. His academic journey is deeply rooted in the exploration of the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As an educator, he imparts knowledge on Internet Studies, drawing from his extensive research on the theory of information, communication, and society. Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the field, as evidenced by his numerous publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly work includes highly cited papers such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have garnered 398 and 278 citations respectively. His research interests are diverse and include social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science, with a particular focus on how false information propagates within the hybrid news system. His recent endeavors delve into coordinated link sharing behavior during the Italian elections, reflecting his commitment to understanding media manipulation and its societal impacts.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in current research projects and collaborations that explore the dynamics of digital communication. He frequently shares insights and updates on his ongoing work through professional platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he connects with peers and contributes to the discourse on pressing issues in digital media studies. His active presence in the academic community is complemented by his participation in various conferences and workshops, where he continues to expand his research on the digital ecosystem.

With a total of 20 publications and 1762 citations to his credit, Professor Giglietto remains a prominent voice in the field of Internet Studies. His dedication to advancing knowledge on the interplay between technology and society underscores his role as a thought leader and educator, inspiring the next generation of scholars in the ever-evolving landscape of digital communication.

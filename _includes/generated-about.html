Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, his work predominantly explores the dynamic interactions between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, featuring in prestigious publications such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the intricate mechanics of media manipulation and misinformation within digital ecosystems. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," acclaimed for its insightful analysis into participatory media, and the influential study "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system." These studies, among others, have garnered significant citations, reflecting his impact in the field of Internet and communication studies.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in pioneering research projects that examine the role of social media in shaping public discourse and its implications for democratic processes. His participation in international conferences and collaborations with interdisciplinary teams highlight his commitment to advancing knowledge in computational social science and disinformation analysis. Additionally, he actively shares insights and ongoing developments in his research through platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, enriching the academic and professional community with his findings.

Through his continued dedication to teaching and research, Fabio Giglietto remains at the forefront of exploring how digital technologies influence societal structures. His work not only contributes to academic discourse but also informs public understanding of the complex landscape of modern communication.

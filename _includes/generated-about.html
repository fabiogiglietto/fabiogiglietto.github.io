Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a dedicated focus on the intersection of social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has established himself as a leading figure in the field of Internet Studies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, exploring how digital platforms influence and are influenced by social dynamics.

Professor Giglietto's extensive publication record includes notable works such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," which highlight his commitment to understanding the complex media landscape. His research has been featured in prominent journals like the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society, contributing significantly to the academic discourse on media manipulation and disinformation.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that scrutinize coordinated link sharing behavior during pivotal events like the Italian elections. His work often employs computational social science methods to analyze social media data, providing insights into the mechanics of digital communication. His ongoing research is bolstered by recent collaborations and professional engagements that reflect his dynamic presence in the field.

Through his teaching and research, Professor Giglietto continues to influence both scholars and practitioners, fostering a deeper understanding of the digital world's impact on society.

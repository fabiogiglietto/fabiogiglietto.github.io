Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is an integral part of the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. As an expert in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto's research keenly focuses on the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work has garnered significant attention, with numerous publications in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's prolific research contributions include influential studies like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." These publications, among others, have collectively amassed over 1,700 citations, underscoring his impact in the field. His ongoing research delves into the complexities of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science, reflecting a commitment to understanding the evolving landscape of digital communication.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community through various platforms. His presence on BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn offers insights into his current projects and collaborations, including recent investigations into coordinated media manipulation and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems. His dedication to advancing the field is further demonstrated by his involvement in cutting-edge research initiatives and collaborations with international scholars.

Through his teaching and research, Fabio Giglietto continues to contribute significantly to the discourse on digital communication and media studies. His work not only advances academic understanding but also informs public policy and societal debates, making him a pivotal figure in the field of Internet Studies.

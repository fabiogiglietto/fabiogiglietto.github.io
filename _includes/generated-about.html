Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. Renowned for his thorough research and expertise, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the fields of information theory, communication, and society, with a particular emphasis on the dynamic interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies.

Throughout his academic career, Professor Giglietto has published extensively in leading journals, such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society. His influential works, including studies on second screen participation and the circulation of false information within hybrid news systems, have garnered considerable citations, underscoring their impact on the academic community. His research continues to explore the limits and possibilities of using social media platforms as research data sources, contributing valuable insights into the evolving nature of digital communication.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is advancing his research on social media analysis, focusing on disinformation and computational social science. His recent projects delve into coordinated link-sharing behavior during elections, a critical area of study in understanding media manipulation. Active on platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, he frequently shares his latest findings and engages with the academic community, fostering discussions on contemporary challenges in media and communication studies.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to his field is further exemplified by his involvement in various research initiatives and collaborations, reflecting his commitment to addressing pressing societal issues through rigorous academic inquiry. His work not only enriches the academic landscape but also provides valuable perspectives for understanding the complex interrelations between media, technology, and society in the digital age.

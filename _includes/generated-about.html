Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound dedication to the exploration of how social systems, media, and digital technologies interact, Dr. Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in the field of Internet Studies. His work primarily focuses on the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has contributed significantly to the academic community through his extensive publications in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's research is characterized by a pioneering approach to understanding the dynamics of social media and digital communication. He has published widely on topics such as media manipulation and disinformation, with notable works including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system." His research has garnered considerable attention, amassing over 1,700 citations across 20 publications, highlighting the impact and relevance of his contributions to the field.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Dr. Giglietto is actively involved in several cutting-edge research projects that delve into computational social science and the analysis of social media ecosystems. His recent work examines coordinated link sharing behavior and its implications for media integrity during significant political events. Dr. Giglietto is also a prominent figure on professional networks such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, where he shares insights on his latest research endeavors and engages with the scholarly community. His commitment to advancing the understanding of digital communication continues to influence both academic and public discourse.

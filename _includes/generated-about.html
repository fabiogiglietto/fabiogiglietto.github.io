Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic foundation and a passion for understanding the intersections of media, digital technologies, and social systems, Fabio has established himself as a thought leader in the field of communication studies. His research delves into the complex dynamics of information dissemination, particularly within the context of social media and its impact on society.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to the academic discourse through extensive publications in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His notable works include the highly cited "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His scholarly contributions have been recognized globally, amassing a total of 1,763 citations across 20 publications, showcasing his influence in the field.

In addition to his academic pursuits, Fabio is actively engaged in current research projects that explore the role of social media in propagating disinformation and the implications of coordinated media manipulation, as evidenced by his recent work on the Italian elections. His keen interest in the evolving landscape of computational social science positions him at the forefront of contemporary debates about digital communication. Through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, Fabio shares insights and engages with the broader academic community, furthering discussions on the future of internet studies.

As a dedicated educator and researcher, Fabio Giglietto continues to inspire and mentor students and colleagues alike at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His commitment to advancing knowledge in internet studies not only contributes to academic excellence but also informs societal understanding of media's role in shaping our world. Fabio's ongoing projects and collaborations reflect his unwavering dedication to exploring the intricacies of digital communication and its societal ramifications.

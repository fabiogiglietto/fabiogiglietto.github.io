Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. An influential figure in the field, he delves deeply into the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular emphasis on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly work is widely recognized, having been published in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

With a robust portfolio of over 20 publications and a total of 1747 citations, Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the understanding of digital communication dynamics. His notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered 397 citations, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," cited 276 times. His research continues to explore the intricate ways false information propagates within hybrid news systems, as reflected in his influential paper, "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system."

Professor Giglietto remains actively engaged in cutting-edge research, focusing on social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. He has recently been involved in projects analyzing coordinated link-sharing behavior during elections, shedding light on media manipulation tactics. Through his dynamic presence on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, Threads, and LinkedIn, he shares insights into his ongoing research endeavors and collaborations, making significant strides in understanding and combating digital misinformation.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is not only a dedicated educator but also a leading voice in discussions on the societal impacts of digital technologies. His commitment to exploring the complexities of digital information flow and its implications for society underscores his role as an essential contributor to the academic community and beyond. As he continues to advance his research and teaching activities, Professor Giglietto remains at the forefront of internet studies, contributing to the broader dialogue on technology and society in the digital age.

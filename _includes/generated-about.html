Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic foundation, Professor Giglietto has dedicated his career to exploring the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work primarily focuses on the theory of information, communication, and society, positioning him as a leading figure in understanding how digital platforms influence public discourse and social interactions.

Professor Giglietto's prolific research output is evidenced by his extensive publications in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Among his notable works are "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," which collectively have garnered significant academic attention and citations. His research not only advances theoretical understandings but also offers critical insights into the practical implications of media manipulation, misinformation, and coordinated link sharing behaviors observed during major political events.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge projects that delve into the dynamics of social media analysis, disinformation, and computational social science. His recent endeavors include investigating the role of digital platforms in shaping public opinion and the mechanisms through which false information proliferates across networks. Active on professional networks such as BlueSky and LinkedIn, he frequently shares insights from his latest research, contributing to broader discussions on the ethical and societal impacts of digital communication technologies.

Outside of his research, Professor Giglietto is committed to educating the next generation of scholars and practitioners in Internet Studies. Through his teaching at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he inspires students to critically engage with the digital landscape, equipping them with the skills necessary to navigate and influence an ever-evolving media environment. His dedication to both scholarship and pedagogy underscores his role as a pivotal contributor to the academic community and beyond.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he is a pivotal figure in the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a profound passion for understanding the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Professor Giglietto has dedicated his career to exploring the theory of information, communication, and society. His academic journey is marked by extensive publications in prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the dynamic interplay between digital platforms and societal behaviors, with a keen interest in the phenomenon of disinformation and media manipulation. His notable works, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," have garnered significant citations, reflecting their impact on the field. His ongoing projects continue to push the boundaries of social media analysis and computational social science, with a focus on how coordinated link-sharing behaviors influence public discourse, particularly during electoral processes.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community through platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, sharing insights from his latest research projects and collaborations. His current work often highlights the challenges and possibilities of using digital platforms as research data sources. He remains a prominent voice in discussions on the ethical use of social media data, contributing to the academic and broader public understanding of these critical issues.

Professor Giglietto's commitment to advancing knowledge in his field is further evidenced by his active participation in international conferences and workshops, where he frequently presents his research findings. His contributions have not only enriched the academic community but have also provided valuable frameworks for understanding the complexities of media and technology in contemporary society. With a total of 20 publications and 1763 citations, Fabio Giglietto continues to be a leading authority in his discipline, inspiring both colleagues and students alike.

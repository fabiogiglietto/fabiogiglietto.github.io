Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound dedication to understanding the intricate dynamics of information, communication, and society, his research primarily explores the complex relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Over the years, Professor Giglietto has contributed extensively to scholarly discourse, with his work published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's seminal research includes pivotal studies like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant attention with 397 citations. His exploration of the potential and limitations of social media platforms as research data sources, as detailed in "The open laboratory," has become a cornerstone in the field. Current projects continue to delve into the mechanics of information dissemination, particularly focusing on the phenomena of disinformation and media manipulation during critical events, such as elections.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in advancing the field of computational social science, with a focus on social media analysis and the spread of false information within hybrid news systems. His recent endeavors include investigating coordinated link-sharing behaviors that occurred during the Italian elections of 2018 and 2019. He remains a thought leader in the field, frequently sharing insights and updates on his ongoing research via platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, where he engages with a vibrant community of scholars and professionals.

In recognition of his significant contributions to the academic community, Professor Giglietto has received numerous accolades and continues to secure prestigious grants to support his cutting-edge research. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he not only leads transformative research initiatives but also mentors the next generation of scholars, fostering a rich environment for academic growth and innovation.

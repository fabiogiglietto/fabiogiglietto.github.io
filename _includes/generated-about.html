Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a renowned Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has established himself as a leading voice in the field of information, communication, and society theories. His academic journey is marked by his dedication to exploring how digital platforms shape contemporary public discourse and influence social behavior.

Dr. Giglietto's prolific research output includes numerous publications in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. His work on topics like the second screen phenomena, media manipulation during elections, and the circulation of false information has garnered significant attention, with a total of 1,751 citations across 20 publications. Notable among his works is his 2014 publication on "Second Screen and Participation," which has received nearly 400 citations, highlighting its impact on the field.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research projects that delve into the dynamics of social media analysis and disinformation, with a particular interest in the computational aspects of social science. His recent endeavors include examining coordinated link-sharing behaviors during elections and the role of hashtags in shaping public narratives. Dr. Giglietto actively shares insights and engages with the academic community through platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, where he discusses the latest findings and developments in his research areas.

Beyond his research, Dr. Giglietto is deeply committed to teaching and mentoring students in the field of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His courses provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the digital landscape and its societal implications, preparing the next generation of scholars to tackle the challenges of a rapidly evolving media environment. As an influential figure in his field, Dr. Giglietto continues to contribute to the academic discourse, furthering our understanding of the digital world's role in shaping modern society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound commitment to understanding the intersection of social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto's research primarily explores the theory of information, communication, and society. He has significantly contributed to scholarly discourse, with his work being published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Dr. Giglietto's research is characterized by a keen interest in the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science. His influential publications, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," have garnered widespread academic attention and citations. With a total of 20 publications and 1,761 citations, his work continues to shape the understanding of media manipulation and coordinated online behavior.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is engaged in cutting-edge research projects that examine the role of digital platforms in shaping public discourse. His expertise is frequently sought after, and he actively shares insights from his research on professional networks like LinkedIn and academic forums. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he not only leads innovative research but also imparts his knowledge to students through courses in Internet Studies. Dr. Giglietto's ongoing contributions to the field underscore his dedication to advancing the understanding of digital communication's impact on society.

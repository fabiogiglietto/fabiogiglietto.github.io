Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he contributes to the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. Dr. Giglietto's academic pursuits are deeply rooted in exploring the intricate dynamics between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, emphasizing the evolving relationships shaped by the digital age. This work has led to significant contributions to journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, among others.

Throughout his career, Dr. Giglietto has consistently explored the impact of digital platforms on societal discourse and media manipulation. His noteworthy publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered widespread attention with 398 citations. Another key work, "The Open Laboratory: Limits and Possibilities of Using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a Research Data Source," highlights his pioneering efforts in utilizing digital platforms for research, earning 278 citations. In more recent research, Dr. Giglietto has focused on the role of coordinated link sharing behavior during key political events, a testament to his continued engagement with timely issues in media studies.

In addition to his prolific academic contributions, Dr. Giglietto actively shares insights on contemporary issues in social media analysis and disinformation through his professional social media presence, particularly on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn. His recent research projects continue to address the challenges posed by the digital transformation of news systems and the spread of misinformation. Through his teaching and research, Dr. Giglietto not only advances academic discourse but also contributes to a broader understanding of the digital landscape's impact on communication and society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey has been marked by a profound exploration of the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies. As a leading figure in this dynamic field, Professor Giglietto has contributed significantly to scholarly discourse, with his work appearing in prominent journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the complexities of digital communication, analyzing phenomena such as social media manipulation and the spread of misinformation within hybrid news systems. His notable publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have collectively amassed over 1,700 citations. His work on coordinated link sharing behavior during the Italian elections and discursive strategies post-Charlie Hebdo shooting further underscores his commitment to understanding the societal impacts of digital platforms.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that explore social media analysis and computational social science, utilizing cutting-edge methodologies to uncover insights into the digital landscape. His involvement with platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn reveals an ongoing interest in the effects of disinformation and the evolution of digital communication strategies. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he continues to inspire the next generation of scholars through his teaching and mentorship, while also contributing to the broader academic community through his ongoing research endeavors.

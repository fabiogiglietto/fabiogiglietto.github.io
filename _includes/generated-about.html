Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. At the forefront of digital research, he has carved out a niche in understanding the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research delves into the theory of information, communication, and society, contributing significantly to the academic discourse with articles published in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's work is characterized by an in-depth analysis of social media dynamics, especially concerning disinformation and the manipulation of media narratives. His notable publications include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has been cited extensively, reflecting its impact on the field. With a total of 20 publications and 1763 citations, his research continues to influence scholars and practitioners alike.

Currently, Fabio Giglietto is engaged in innovative research projects that explore the complexities of computational social science, with a specific focus on how misinformation spreads within digital environments. Through his active presence on professional networks, he shares insights on these pressing issues, contributing to a broader understanding of how digital platforms shape public discourse. His ongoing projects and analyses remain at the cutting edge of internet studies, offering critical perspectives on the role of technology in contemporary society.

Professor Giglietto's academic journey is complemented by his commitment to education, as he imparts knowledge on Internet Studies to his students at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His dedication to both research and teaching underscores his role as a thought leader in the field, continually pushing the boundaries of how we understand and interact with digital media.

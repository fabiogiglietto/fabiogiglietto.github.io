Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, where he contributes to the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies. With a profound interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his work predominantly explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His insightful research has been featured in esteemed journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Throughout his academic career, Professor Giglietto has significantly advanced the understanding of digital communication's role in modern society. His notable publication, "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," has garnered considerable attention with 397 citations, reflecting his impact on the field. His research often involves examining the dynamics of social media platforms as research data sources and exploring the dissemination of false information within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in projects that scrutinize coordinated media manipulation behaviors, especially during pivotal electoral periods. His work in this area, such as the study on link sharing behavior during the Italian elections, underscores the societal implications of digital media usage and disinformation. Engaged with the academic community, he frequently shares insights on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, discussing recent advancements in computational social science and the challenges posed by misinformation.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing his field is further evidenced by his involvement in various professional activities and his continuous effort to mentor the next generation of scholars. With 20 publications and 1,747 citations to his name, he remains a pivotal figure in the discourse surrounding the intersection of media, technology, and society.

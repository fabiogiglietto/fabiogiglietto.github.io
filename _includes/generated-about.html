Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As a leading expert in the interplay between social systems, media, and digital technologies, Dr. Giglietto has dedicated his career to exploring the intricate dynamics of information, communication, and society. His work is prominently published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication and Information, Communication & Society, reflecting his significant contributions to the field.

Dr. Giglietto's research interests are centered around the theory of information and the societal implications of digital media. His scholarly endeavors have resulted in 20 publications, amassing a total of 1740 citations. Notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," both of which highlight his methodological innovations and insights into digital communication.

Currently, Dr. Giglietto is deeply engaged in research examining the role of social media in the dissemination of disinformation and the computational analysis of social trends. His recent projects include a study on coordinated link sharing behavior during Italian elections, underscoring his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by digital misinformation. Actively participating in academic discussions, he frequently shares his insights on platforms such as BlueSky and Mastodon, where he discusses the latest developments in his field.

Dr. Giglietto's academic excellence and impactful research have positioned him as a pivotal figure in Internet studies. Through his teaching and continued research, he strives to equip the next generation of scholars with the tools necessary to navigate and critically assess the rapidly evolving digital landscape. His work not only advances academic knowledge but also provides valuable perspectives on the role of digital media in contemporary society.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is renowned for his expertise in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. Prof. Giglietto's extensive body of work has been widely published, featuring in prominent journals including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, Social Media + Society, and Social Science Computer Review.

Throughout his academic career, Prof. Giglietto has made substantial contributions to the understanding of digital communication phenomena. Among his notable publications are "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant attention with 397 citations, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," cited 276 times. His research delves into the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science, providing critical insights into the manipulation of media and the spread of false information within hybrid news systems.

Currently, Prof. Giglietto is actively engaged in research projects that explore the implications of coordinated link-sharing behavior during political events, as exemplified by his work on the 2018 and 2019 Italian elections. His scholarly pursuits are supported by a robust digital presence, where he shares his latest findings and engages with the academic community on platforms such as BlueSky, Mastodon, Threads, and LinkedIn. Through these channels, he continues to contribute to the discourse on social media analysis and the evolving landscape of digital communication.

Prof. Giglietto's ongoing dedication to advancing the field of Internet Studies is evident in his commitment to teaching and research at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His work not only enriches academic understanding but also informs policy and practice in the digital age. With a total of 20 publications and 1751 citations, his contributions have significantly impacted the field, earning him recognition as a leading scholar in his area of expertise.

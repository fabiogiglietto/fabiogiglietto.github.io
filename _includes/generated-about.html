Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. In his academic role, he is dedicated to teaching and exploring Internet Studies, with a particular focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research interests encompass the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has made significant contributions to understanding how these elements interact in the digital age.

Throughout his career, Professor Giglietto has published extensively in numerous prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society. Some of his notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," which have garnered hundreds of citations and reflect his commitment to advancing the field of social media analysis and disinformation studies. His research has been instrumental in shedding light on the dynamics of information dissemination and media manipulation, particularly during pivotal events such as the Italian elections.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community and the public through various platforms, including BlueSky and Mastodon, where he shares insights from his ongoing research. His current projects delve into the computational analysis of social media data, exploring the spread of disinformation and the societal impact of digital communication technologies. His work not only contributes to academic discourse but also informs practical approaches to media literacy and digital communication strategies.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Fabio Giglietto continues to inspire the next generation of scholars through his teaching and mentorship. His dedication to research excellence and his ability to bridge theoretical and practical insights make him a leading figure in the ever-evolving field of Internet Studies.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a robust academic background, he specializes in the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His extensive research portfolio includes significant contributions to the theory of information, communication, and society, with his work frequently appearing in prestigious journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research endeavors primarily focus on the dynamics of social media analysis, disinformation, and the broader field of computational social science. His notable publications, including "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," have received widespread recognition, amassing over 1,748 citations collectively. His recent projects continue to explore the impact of coordinated link-sharing behavior during significant political events, further establishing his expertise in the realm of media manipulation and hybrid news systems.

Beyond his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the scholarly community through various platforms, sharing insights on the evolving landscape of digital communication. His current research projects delve into the mechanisms of disinformation dissemination and strategies for combating fake news, reflecting his commitment to addressing pressing societal challenges. Engaging with his audience on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, and LinkedIn, he remains at the forefront of discussions surrounding the future of media and information in the digital age.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto is not only recognized for his research but also for his contributions to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies. His dedication to bridging theoretical concepts with practical applications continues to inspire students and colleagues alike, fostering a vibrant academic community dedicated to understanding the complexities of our digital world.

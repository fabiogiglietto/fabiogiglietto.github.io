Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. In this role, he not only teaches but also delves into the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research is deeply rooted in the theory of information, communication, and society, and he has contributed significantly to the field through extensive publications in renowned journals including the Journal of Communication and Information, Communication & Society.

Professor Giglietto's scholarly work is widely recognized, with a total of 20 publications and 1761 citations. Some of his notable works include "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered 398 citations, and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," cited 278 times. His recent projects continue to explore the dynamics of social media, disinformation, and computational social science, reflecting his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges of the digital age.

In addition to his academic contributions, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged with the academic community through his social media presence on platforms like BlueSky, Mastodon, Threads, and LinkedIn, where he shares insights and developments related to his ongoing research. His work on the hybrid news system and media manipulation during elections highlights his focus on the societal implications of digital communication technologies. Through these platforms, he also connects with fellow researchers and the larger public, fostering a dialogue on critical issues pertaining to internet studies.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He is recognized for his extensive work in the field of Internet Studies, where he explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research is deeply rooted in the theory of information, communication, and society, and has been published in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto has made significant contributions to the academic community with over 20 publications and a total of 1761 citations. His influential work includes studies on media manipulation, digital participation, and the propagation of false information within digital spaces. Notable publications, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," have garnered substantial attention, with nearly 400 citations. His research continues to address pressing issues in media and communication, with a recent focus on the dynamics of disinformation and the computational analysis of social media.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Fabio Giglietto is not only a dedicated educator teaching Internet Studies but also a proactive researcher involved in several innovative projects. His current endeavors include analyzing coordinated link-sharing behaviors in political contexts and exploring the hybrid news system's response to fake news. His recent activities on platforms such as BlueSky and LinkedIn reflect his ongoing commitment to understanding the evolving landscape of digital communication and its societal implications.

Professor Giglietto's work is pivotal in navigating the complexities of digital media environments, and his contributions continue to influence both academic discourse and practical applications in the field of Internet Studies. Through his research, teaching, and public engagement, he remains at the forefront of exploring the impact of digital technologies on contemporary society.

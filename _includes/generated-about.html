Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. He teaches Internet Studies and Social Media Analysis. His research explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies, with a focus on the theory of information, communication, and society.

Professor Giglietto's work delves into the impact of digital platforms on social interaction, disinformation, and political communication. His research projects involve social media analysis, computational social science, and the detection of disinformation campaigns. He has published extensively in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, the Journal of Broadcasting and Electronic Media, Social Media + Society. His publications include impactful works on second screen participation, open laboratory methodologies using social media data, coordinated link-sharing behavior during elections, and the circulation of false information. He actively contributes to the academic community through discussions on platforms like BlueSky and LinkedIn, exploring trends in digital technology and the implications of social media dynamics.

Professor Giglietto is involved in several research initiatives. He is the Principal Investigator of the Mapping Italian News (MINE) Research Program, and a work package leader in the PROMPT project, an EU-funded initiative focused on detecting and analyzing disinformation narratives. His work includes investigating coordinated link-sharing behavior and its impact on media manipulation and public opinion. He also explores the state of social media research APIs and tools in the Digital Services Act (DSA) era.

His research employs an integrated approach to understanding the interplay between manipulative actors, deceptive behavior, and harmful content.

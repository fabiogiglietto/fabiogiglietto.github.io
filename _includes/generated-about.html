Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. As a leading scholar in the field, he specializes in the theory of information, communication, and society, with a particular focus on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a robust academic portfolio, Professor Giglietto has contributed extensively to renowned journals, including the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research is at the forefront of understanding digital media's impact on society, emphasizing social media analysis and disinformation. His notable publications, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "‘Fake news’ is the invention of a liar: How false information circulates within the hybrid news system," have garnered significant citations, reflecting his influence in the field. With an impressive total of 20 publications and 1747 citations, his work continues to shape the discourse on media and society.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto actively engages in cutting-edge research projects that explore the dynamics of online communication and its societal implications. His recent endeavors on social media platforms, including BlueSky and Mastodon, reveal a keen interest in computational social science and the evolving landscape of digital interactions. Through these efforts, he remains committed to advancing knowledge on how digital technologies can both inform and misinform the public, a topic of vital importance in today's interconnected world.

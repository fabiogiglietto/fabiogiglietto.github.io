Fabio Giglietto, Ph.D., is a Full Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a deep-seated interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto focuses on the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His work critically examines how these elements interact and influence societal dynamics, a subject on which he has extensively published in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research portfolio boasts significant contributions to the field, including the highly cited "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source." His recent work delves into the complexities of media manipulation and disinformation, as evidenced by his publication, "It takes a village to manipulate the media: coordinated link sharing behavior during 2018 and 2019 Italian elections." These studies not only underline his expertise but also reflect his commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges posed by digital media in contemporary society.

Beyond his publications, Professor Giglietto actively engages in the academic community through his involvement in various research projects related to social media analysis and computational social science. His current interests are reflected in his online presence, where he shares insights and developments in the field via platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon. His dedication to advancing the study of digital communication is further supported by recent grants and collaborations that explore the impact of disinformation on public discourse.

As a leader in Internet Studies, Professor Giglietto continues to influence both academic circles and public understanding of the digital age. His work not only advances theoretical frameworks but also provides practical insights into the navigation of an increasingly interconnected digital world. At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, he remains a pivotal figure in fostering an enriched understanding of digital media's role in shaping modern communication landscapes.

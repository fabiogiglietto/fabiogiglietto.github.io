Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is a distinguished Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. His academic journey is deeply rooted in exploring the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. With a profound interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, Professor Giglietto has extensively published in prestigious journals, including the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society, contributing significantly to the field of Internet Studies.

Professor Giglietto's research delves into the dynamics of social media interaction and the dissemination of information in the digital age. His notable works, such as "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets" and "The open laboratory: Limits and possibilities of using Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as a research data source," highlight his commitment to understanding how digital platforms influence public discourse and media manipulation. His comprehensive body of work has garnered over 1,762 citations, reflecting the impact and relevance of his research in contemporary discussions on media and technology.

Currently, Professor Giglietto is actively engaged in research projects that investigate the phenomena of disinformation and coordinated link-sharing behavior in political contexts, as evidenced by his study on the 2018 and 2019 Italian elections. His dedication to computational social science is also evident in his recent explorations of data-driven methodologies to analyze social media trends and their implications on societal communication practices. Through platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon, he continues to share insights and contribute to ongoing conversations about the ever-evolving landscape of digital communication.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Professor Giglietto not only advances academic research but also mentors emerging scholars in Internet Studies. His role as an educator is complemented by his active participation in international conferences and collaborative research initiatives that aim to address the challenges posed by digital misinformation. As a thought leader in his field, Fabio Giglietto remains committed to fostering an informed understanding of how digital technologies reshape communication and societal interactions.

Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an esteemed Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a keen focus on the theory of information, communication, and society, he explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His scholarly contributions have been widely recognized, with numerous publications in top-tier journals such as the Journal of Communication and Social Media + Society.

Professor Giglietto's research endeavors are at the forefront of understanding digital communication dynamics, particularly the role of social media in shaping public discourse. His notable work includes a comprehensive analysis of coordinated link sharing behaviors during the Italian elections and insightful studies on the circulation of disinformation within hybrid news systems. With a publication record of 20 papers and a total of 1,748 citations, his work significantly influences both academic and public policy debates.

In addition to his academic achievements, Professor Giglietto actively engages with the broader research community through various social media platforms, sharing insights and developments in his field. Recent projects include examining the impact of digital platforms on electoral processes and the spread of 'fake news.' His active presence on platforms like BlueSky and Mastodon provides a dynamic channel for discussing the latest findings and collaborations in social media analysis and computational social science.

Professor Giglietto's dedication to advancing knowledge in internet studies is further evidenced by his involvement in recent grants and professional activities that explore the intersection of digital technologies and societal change. His role as an Associate Professor at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo continues to inspire students and colleagues alike, fostering an environment of innovation and critical inquiry.

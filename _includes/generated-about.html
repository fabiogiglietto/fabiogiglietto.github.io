Fabio Giglietto, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Internet Studies at the Department of Communication Sciences, Humanities and International Studies at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo. With a profound interest in the theory of information, communication, and society, his work primarily explores the intricate relationships between social systems, media, and digital technologies. His research has been widely recognized and published in leading journals such as the Journal of Communication, Information, Communication & Society, and Social Media + Society.

Prof. Giglietto's contributions to the field are exemplified by his extensive body of work, including notable publications like "Second Screen and Participation: A Content Analysis on a Full Season Dataset of Tweets," which has garnered significant citations. His research on the role of social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube as research data sources has also been influential, offering insights into the open laboratory of social media. Currently, his work addresses issues of media manipulation and disinformation, particularly in the context of electoral processes.

In addition to his academic endeavors, Prof. Giglietto is actively engaged in various professional activities and collaborations. His recent projects delve into computational social science, focusing on the analysis of social media behaviors and the spread of misinformation. His participation in international conferences and his presence on professional networks such as LinkedIn and BlueSky demonstrate his commitment to advancing the field and engaging with the global academic community.

At the University of Urbino Carlo Bo, Prof. Giglietto is dedicated to educating the next generation of scholars in Internet Studies, fostering a critical understanding of digital communication's impact on society. His passion for teaching and research continues to inspire students and colleagues alike, contributing to the vibrant academic environment of his department.
